# Realismo mágico (Magical Realism)

## Definition

Magical realism is a literary mode in which fantastic or supernatural elements are presented as matter-of-fact within an otherwise realistic narrative world. Associated most prominently with Latin American fiction (García Márquez, Borges, Rulfo) but present in European and world literature more broadly (Kafka, Grass, Grass, Bulgakov, Woolf). [unsourced — verify: no academic source on magical realism yet ingested.]

Within this wiki, the term appears as an analogy, not a genre classification: [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) invokes magical realism cautiously ("ya me perdonarán" — "forgive me") to characterise the *type* of imagination at work in Gospodínov, not to classify him as a magical realist author per se.
