# Literatura de viajes (Travel Literature)

## Definition

A literary tradition in which the movement through space — foreign countries, remote routes, unfamiliar cultures — serves as the occasion and frame for personal and moral examination. Distinguished from tourist writing (descriptive, celebratory) and from historical-geographical survey by its investment in the traveller's inner life and its capacity to sustain philosophical or emotional reflection through displacement.

Within this wiki, the term appears primarily through [Gonzalo Torné's](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) sceptical assessment: the genre is difficult to reanimate in contemporary fiction. His reading of Damon Galgut's work suggests a key distinction between *travel* (going far away) and *displacement* (being out of place, protagonising a life not recognised as one's own) — with the latter being the more productive literary subject.

## Critical Treatments

- [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) closes his Galgut review with the observation: "how difficult it is to reanimate the travel literature genre." This implies a genre exhaustion he does not develop but registers as a critical verdict. The displacement/travel distinction is his main contribution to the theme in the wiki.
