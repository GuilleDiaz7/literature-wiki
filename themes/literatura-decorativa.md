# Literatura decorativa (Decorative Literature)

## Definition

"Literatura decorativa" is a critical term used by [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) (see [source](../sources/2026-03-09_albion-anna-hope.md)) to describe fiction that is technically accomplished but ornamental in purpose — that invokes the settings, atmospheres, or conventions of a serious literary tradition without the moral nerve, ironic intelligence, or formal ambition that gave the original tradition its value.

The concept does not denote a formal category but a critical evaluation: decorative literature is measured against the tradition it cites and found to offer pleasure in place of confrontation, nostalgia in place of inquiry. It is competent craft that delivers what the target reader expects, efficiently, from the first page — but which constitutes what Torné calls a "leve falsificación" (mild falsification) of the literature it takes as its model.

The term is applied primarily to contemporary fiction that trades on the prestige of canonical precedents.

## Critical Treatments

- [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) is the sole source for this concept in the wiki so far. His formulation: great literature is great because of "el atrevimiento con el que se decidió a confrontar la complejidad de la vida" — the daring with which it confronted the complexity of life. Decorative literature lacks this daring and thereby mildly falsifies its own tradition.
