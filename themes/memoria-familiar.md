# Memoria familiar (Family Memory)

## Definition

The literary reconstruction of intimate, private, discontinuous family memory — as distinct from official history (political, economic, military) — through the materials memory leaves behind: photographs, objects, stories, silences, and absences. This kind of memory is fragile (few documents, enigmatic even when lived through), but "more intense and sensitive" than the "formidable and strident registers" of public history.

The literary treatment of family memory in this mode is neither nostalgic nor narrative-driven; it is closer to the palaeontologist's work — reconstructing something vast and alive from small, fragmentary remains. The photo album functions not only as a register but as a "Ouija board" — a medium for voices that never fully pass from memory.

Within this wiki, the concept is defined through [Gonzalo Torné's](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) reading of Ana Gorría.

## Critical Treatments

- [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): *Tiempo profundo* "traces a poetics of intimate, discontinuous family memories" — fragile relative to official history but more intense. The palaeontologist comparison: from a jaw fragment, the whole dinosaur is reimagined. The photo album as Ouija board: voices that never fully pass.

## Cross-References
