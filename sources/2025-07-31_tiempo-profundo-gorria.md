## Summary

[Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) opens by identifying *Tiempo profundo* as a book that "follows neither the codes of commercial literature nor those of creative writing schools" and does not fit evidently into an available genre — a species of book that deserves maximum critical attention and gratitude. He describes it as "delicate and wise," offering the pleasure of advancing through pages "where what will come next is never predictable" — not through cheap narrative intrigue but through the deployment of literary imagination.

The book proposes a double movement: the reconstruction of Ana Gorría's family memory (grandmother, father, house, neighbourhood, dog, siblings) through the family photo album; while in parallel offering an essay-study on how memory functions — its triggers and structures — through "very vivid and precise" comments on the work of more than a dozen female photographers.

Torné rates the essays as first-category, but locates the book's emotional wingspan in the passages where the narrator reconstructs "fragments of stories, character profiles, recounts of losses and absences (some as subtle as a disappearance to another continent, others as spectacular as a burnt house) with the help of a family album that is as much a register as a Ouija board, for its power to summon voices that never pass in memory."

He characterises the book's poetics as "a poetics of intimate, discontinuous family memories" — nodding to his own generation's experience of having few photographs, small nodules that incite reconstruction of long undocumented periods "enigmatic even though we lived through them, like the palaeontologist who reimagines a formidable dinosaur from a jaw fragment." These memories are fragile compared to the "formidable and strident registers of political, economic, or military history, but more intense and sensitive."

The review ends celebrating the book's double success: it is particularly conscious of the body's weight and fragility, of the fatigue of work and the monotony of commuting, of friend networks as new constructed families — but also proposes a kind of immateriality, a limbo of age where looking at the album makes years attenuate. "Not so much a re-entering of childhood — the eyes preserve accumulated experience — it is only that where the body ages, consciousness matures."

## Key Claims

- *Tiempo profundo* belongs to neither commercial fiction nor creative writing school conventions — an independent literary achievement deserving special attention.
- Double structure: family memory through photo album + essays on female photographers.
- The family album functions as both register and Ouija board: summons voices that never fully pass in memory.
- The book proposes a poetics of intimate, discontinuous family memory — fragile relative to official history but more intense.
- Final paradox: looking at the album attenuates the years — not infantilisation but a maturing of consciousness alongside an ageing body.

## Limitations or Biases

Torné's review is warmly and almost exclusively positive. The limitation is the review's brevity on the essay half of the book (he calls it "first-category" without developing why). The book's engagement with female photographers — which might be the most critically productive dimension — is mentioned but not analysed.
