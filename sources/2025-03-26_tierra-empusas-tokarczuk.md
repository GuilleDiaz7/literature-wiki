## Summary

[Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) reviews Olga Tokarczuk's *Tierra de empusas*, a novel he reads in explicit contrast to Tokarczuk's masterwork *Los errantes*. Where *Los errantes* withheld its organising keys until the last pages, *Tierra de empusas* does the opposite: its presuppositions and literary operation are visible from the first pages. "The enigma gives way to the programme."

The novel infiltrates Thomas Mann's *La montaña mágica* with a double purpose: parodying the masculinity of the period (the 1913 sanatorium setting) and parodying the most pompous aspects of Mann's prestigious dialogues, while denouncing the erasure of women — both as characters who suffer impune violence rooted in contempt, and as an invisible chorus of observers who "don't miss a beat" and claim to be essential to sustaining the idle recuperation of the magical sanatorium.

Torné acknowledges Tokarczuk's talents — a playful imagination charged with malice, prose plasticity, an allegorical air — and distinguishes the novel from being an impugnation of Mann (it is rather a parodic game). But he notes the inevitable comparisons with Mann, who wrote against his own class's presuppositions with psychological and social tension, fully conscious of recreating an exhausted world overwhelmed by the Great War. No equivalent tension is visible in Tokarczuk's *Tierra de empusas*, which progresses without hesitation in the fulfilment of its plan: "worthy of applause, and with which I agree, phrase by phrase, without the reading reaching me emotionally."

His sharpest critical question: why has Tokarczuk silenced the women of literature in her narrative? If she is playing in Mann's territory — "one of the pinnacles of the canonical novel, last heir and custodian of the great tradition of the 19th century" — why are Flaubert's women absent, or Balzac's, or Galdós's and Clarín's, or Tolstoy's and Dostoyevsky's? And why no female colleagues: Austen, George Eliot, the Brontë sisters? The absence of this overwhelming literary representation of women, even for the spectacularity of the enterprise, gives the reading "a somewhat opportunistic flavour."

## Key Claims

- *Tierra de empusas* is programmatic from the first page — the opposite approach to *Los errantes*.
- The novel parodies Thomas Mann's *La montaña mágica* to denounce historical erasure of women, but lacks Mann's own self-critical tension.
- Torné agrees with the feminist programme frase a frase but is emotionally unmoved.
- Key contradiction: Tokarczuk's erasure-denunciation itself erases the vast literary tradition of female characters by male canonical authors and female authors entirely.
- This "elective erasure" ("borrados electivos") gives the book a somewhat opportunistic flavour.

## Limitations or Biases

Torné's reading privileges formal-critical consistency over political effect. His central objection — the elective erasure of literary women — is formally coherent but may be contested by critics who argue that the satirical target is specifically Mann and his male-modernist tradition, not the entire literary canon. Feminist critics might also dispute whether the presence of canonical female writers would strengthen or dilute Tokarczuk's specific formal argument about male-dominated institutional literature.
