# Günter Grass

## Biographical Context

Born 1927 in Danzig (now Gdańsk, Poland); died 2015 in Lübeck, Germany. Nobel Prize in Literature 1999. Known internationally as the author of *El tambor de hojalata* (*The Tin Drum*, 1959), a foundational work of post-war German literature. ## Style and Technique

The wiki contains only one work by Grass: the posthumous *La estatua*, reviewed by [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) as a "miniature that pays homage to paper." Torné characterises the early Grass as "master of the fable, the narrative overflow, and the deployment of an almost juicy sensoriality" — but does not develop this in depth. The posthumous work is a valediction in miniature form, far from the exuberance of his major fiction.
