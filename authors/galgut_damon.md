## Style and Technique

Per [Gonzalo Torné's review of *En una habitación ajena*](../sources/2024-12-02_habitacion-ajena-galgut.md): Galgut's style is analytical with lyrical crests in descriptions, vaguely sensitive to social differences, attentive to the moral profile traced by stories' impact on an autobiographical narrator-protagonist. The narrator observes his younger self with curiosity but without memory of motivations. First and third novella judged as uneven; second praised as the best — "a fantasy about suspended lives."

## Critical Reputation

Within the wiki, represented by one mixed review (Torné, 2024). Torné praises the second novella warmly but finds the first predictable and the third melodramatic. His closing verdict — "how difficult it is to reanimate the travel literature genre" — is sceptical about the project. [unsourced — verify: No broader critical assessment yet ingested.]
