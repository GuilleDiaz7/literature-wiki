# Imposible decir adiós

## Bibliographic Summary

Spanish edition: *Imposible decir adiós*. Trans. Sunme Yoon. Random House, 256 pp., €21.90. Original Korean title: *Chakbyeolhaji anhneunda* (작별하지 않는다), first published 2021. By [Han Kang](../authors/kang_han.md), Nobel Prize in Literature 2024. The review was published shortly after Han Kang received the Nobel — situating this novel within heightened international attention.

## Synopsis

Two women, Gyeongha and Inseon, build a generous friendship at the limit of temerity, set against the background of historical massacres in Korea. Historical violence — structural and deep, seeking a different political justification in each epoch — is formally suppressed, appearing only through dreams, visions, blurred testimonies, and psychological and somatic pain. A slow, heavy snowfall presides over the narrative as a central atmospheric and symbolic element. (Source: [Torné 2025](../sources/2025-01-13_imposible-decir-adios-kang.md))

## Critical Overview

[Gonzalo Torné's 2025 review](../sources/2025-01-13_imposible-decir-adios-kang.md) describes the novel as a two-sided coin: suppressed historical violence on the reverse; its effects on concrete living individuals on the obverse. He catalogues four formal achievements in order of a reader's successive noticing: (1) precision in describing physical and mental pain; (2) fluid passage between oneiric and tactile realism, dissolving the border between them; (3) a spiral temporal syntax — advancing by retreating, retrospective illumination as important as present action; (4) plasticity in describing nature's structure and relation to bodies (rain, snowflakes, interiors of birds, the malicious snowfall).

Torné's verdict is among his most unqualified in the wiki: the story is "human, hard, tender, and sharpened by nervousness, on the edge of illness and redemption," and formal sophistication here, "almost never," intensifies emotion rather than working against it.
