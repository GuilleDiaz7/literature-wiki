# Física de la tristeza

## Bibliographic Summary

Originally published in Bulgarian in 2011; second novel by [Gueorgui Gospodínov](../authors/gospodinov_gueorgui.md). Spanish edition (*Física de la tristeza*) published by Impedimenta (304 pp., €24.95), translated by María Vútova. This is a recovery/reissue for the Spanish market, not a debut publication. The Bulgarian first edition reportedly sold out in a single day. Gospodínov is better known in the Spanish-speaking world for *El manual del tiempo* (*Time Shelter*), which won the International Booker Prize in 2023. ## Synopsis

The boy protagonist's encounter with a young Minotaur — lonely and alienated in his labyrinth — triggers an empathic hypersensitivity: the capacity to inhabit other subjectivities and transfigure emotionally across space, time, and register. The narration moves freely from the real to the fantastic, from private experience to historical and collective life, from the human to the animal and vegetable kingdoms. The symbolic key is announced clearly: everyone is trapped in labyrinths of strangeness, feeling themselves to be more than they appear. (Source: [Torné 2026](../sources/2026-03-24_fisica-tristeza-gospodinov.md))

## Critical Overview

The wiki contains one critical reading: [Gonzalo Torné's 2026 review](../sources/2026-03-24_fisica-tristeza-gospodinov.md).

Torné's assessment is mixed and precisely calibrated. He identifies Gospodínov's central talent as *imagination*, specifically an imagination gifted at vignettes and paradoxical situations, and notes an affinity with magical realism — though he uses the term cautiously. He identifies three corresponding weaknesses: limited facility for character development; imprecision in narrative scenes; indifference to moral examination.

The review's key critical move is taxonomic: it asks whether the text reads better *as a novel* or *as another form*. As a novel, the symbolic architecture seems "rather simplistic" (*algo simplón*) and the escape from formal obligation feels underdeveloped — a missed opportunity. As an *imaginary field notebook* or *diary of occurrences*, the book gains stature. The same text produces different meaning depending on the formal frame brought to it — as a sunny day tastes different depending on the season. The result is characterised as "literatura del pellizco" — literature of pleasant pinches, offering varied emotional experience in well-crafted scenes without demanding deep immersion.

## Interpretive Controversies

### Controversy: Novel or imaginary field notebook?

- **Position A — Novel** (implied by most reception [unsourced — verify]): *Física de la tristeza* is read and marketed as a novel; its fragmentary structure is treated as a formal choice within the novel form.
- **Position B — Imaginary field notebook** ([Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) — see [source](../sources/2026-03-24_fisica-tristeza-gospodinov.md)): Read as a novel, the book falls short of what a novel could achieve. Read as a cuaderno de campo imaginario — a diary of occurrences — it gains stature. The formal frame applied by the reader changes the meaning and quality of what is read.
- **Status**: Position B is Torné's evaluative refinement. The formal question (is this a novel?) is germane to any discussion of the book's achievement.
