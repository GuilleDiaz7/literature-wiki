# Desfile

## Bibliographic Summary

Spanish edition: *Desfile*. Trans. Catalina Martínez Muñoz. Libros del Asteroide, 184 pp., €18.95. Original English title: *Parade* (2024). By [Rachel Cusk](../authors/cusk_rachel.md), Canadian-British author of the Outline trilogy. [unsourced — verify publication year]

## Synopsis

Four chapters, each with two alternating narrative segments that seem to alternate fiction and testimony, varying in tense, space, gender, and historical period. All are united by a family of related preoccupations (the sabotages of feminine creation) and by a series of artists known only as "G" — a nominal Orlando that mutates throughout the book. The book progresses without concern for genre classification — whether novel or non-fiction. (Source: [Torné 2025](../sources/2025-04-06_desfile-cusk.md))

## Critical Overview

[Gonzalo Torné's 2025 review](../sources/2025-04-06_desfile-cusk.md) identifies the book's subject as the sabotages and self-sabotages of feminine creation — social, political, maternal, ideological, conjugal, artistic, racial — seen from a multiplicity of points of view that Cusk makes parade from first to last page. Torné notes the floating "G" character as "a nominal Orlando" — a reference to Woolf's shape-shifting protagonist.

Torné identifies two major talents. First: Cusk's approach to art — she uses artistic descriptions not as word-paintings but as "launchers for the study of animated and vital situations, frequently violent," bypassing the need for narrative scaffolding. Second, and stronger: her cognitive originality — comparable to Emily Dickinson — she only deploys herself when she has something barely expressed to offer. Her style is dry, analytical, ironic, and almost glacial, yet transmits tenderness through closeness to lived experience.

Torné's assessment is among his most unqualified in the wiki. His closing list of paradoxes — "always original and never extravagant, always mundane and never costumbrista" — functions as a summary of what sustained, formally serious contemporary fiction can do.
