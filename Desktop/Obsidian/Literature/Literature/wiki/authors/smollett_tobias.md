# Tobias Smollett

## Biographical Context

Scottish novelist and satirist, one of the founding figures of the English novel alongside Fielding and Richardson. Known for his picaresque novels (*Roderick Random*, *Peregrine Pickle*, *Humphry Clinker*) and for his translations, travel writing, and historical work. He died in Livorno in 1771 after years of poor health and European travel.

## Major Works

- *The Adventures of Roderick Random* (1748) — picaresque; not reviewed in this wiki
- *The Adventures of Peregrine Pickle* (1751) — not reviewed in this wiki
- *The Expedition of Humphry Clinker* (1771) — epistolary masterpiece; not reviewed in this wiki
- [*Historia y aventuras de un átomo* (1769)](../works/smollett_historia-atomo.md) — reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Picaresque energy, satirical aggression, comic brutalism. The *Atom* represents a formal experiment Smollett attempted but failed (per Torné) to sustain: the radical point of view of a particle passing through human bodies is eventually subordinated to conventional satirical procedure.

## Critical Reputation

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): Smollett as a site of unexploited formal radicalism — the *Atom*'s premise was more daring than its execution.
