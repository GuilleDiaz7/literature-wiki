# John Fowles

## Biographical Context

Born 1926 in Leigh-on-Sea, Essex; died 2005 in Lyme Regis, Dorset. British novelist associated with existentialism, postmodernism (in the chronological sense), and the intellectual novel. Best known for *El mago* (*The Magus*, 1966), *La mujer del teniente francés* (*The French Lieutenant's Woman*, 1969), and *El coleccionista* (*The Collector*, 1963). ## Style and Technique

Per [Gonzalo Torné's review of *El Mago*](../sources/2025-12-31_el-mago-fowles.md): Torné notes, from the critic's perspective, a rough narrative voice, mechanical dialogues, imperfectly incorporated erudite material, and an artificial intrigue that loses interest on rereading. From the reader's perspective — and Torné aligns with the reader here — the novel is irresistibly pleasurable: driven by rhythm, narrative cunning, the morbid attraction of dark magic, and the skill with which Fowles draws the protagonist (and reader) into the labyrinth.

## Scholarly Controversies

### Controversy: Is *El Mago* a postmodern novel?

Per Torné's review: the "postmodern" label applies chronologically but not philosophically, since perspectivism is not a postmodern invention but the essential condition of all fiction since Cervantes. [See the work page [*El Mago*](../works/fowles_el-mago.md) for the full argument.]
