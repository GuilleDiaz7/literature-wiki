# Charles Baudelaire

## Biographical Context

French poet and critic, author of *Les Fleurs du Mal* (1857), prosecuted for obscenity in the same year as Flaubert's *Madame Bovary*. A central figure in the transition from Romanticism to Symbolism and Modernism. His art criticism (*Salons*, essays on Delacroix, Wagner, Poe) was as influential as the poetry.

## Major Works

- *Les Fleurs du Mal* (1857) — not reviewed in this wiki
- *Le Spleen de Paris* (prose poems) — not reviewed in this wiki
- [*Correspondencia Flaubert-Baudelaire*](../works/flaubert-baudelaire_correspondencia.md) — letters; reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Prose-as-intoxication: against Flaubert's impersonality, Baudelaire insists on the self, on sensation, on the transformative power of the poem as subjective event. In the letters to Flaubert, a writer aware of his own difficulty — charming, intelligent, more fragile than the verse suggests.

## Critical Reputation

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): the correspondence with Flaubert illuminates Baudelaire through contrast — prose-as-intoxication against Flaubert's prose-as-architecture.
