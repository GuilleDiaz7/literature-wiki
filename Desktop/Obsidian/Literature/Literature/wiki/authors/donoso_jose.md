# José Donoso

## Biographical Context

Chilean novelist, central figure in the Latin American Boom alongside García Márquez, Vargas Llosa, and Cortázar. Author of *El obsceno pájaro de la noche* (1970) and chronicler of the Boom in *Historia personal del "Boom"* (1972). He spent many years in Spain and wrote a celebrated essay on Jane Austen.

## Major Works

- *El obsceno pájaro de la noche* (1970) — major novel; not reviewed in this wiki
- *Historia personal del "Boom"* (1972) — memoir/chronicle; not reviewed in this wiki
- [Essay on Jane Austen / "miradores del tiempo"](../works/donoso_jane-austen-pensamiento.md) — reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Grotesque, carnivalesque, Gothic-inflected Latin American modernism. In his essay writing, Donoso brings literary-critical intelligence to the question of feminine narrative consciousness — his Jane Austen essay, per Torné, demolishes two standard clichés about Austen with unusual precision.

## Critical Reputation

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): the Jane Austen essay is noted as an example of a novelist writing sharply about another novelist — the practitioner's insight rather than the academic's.
