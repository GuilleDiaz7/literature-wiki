# Gustave Flaubert

## Biographical Context

French novelist, foundational figure of literary realism and of the doctrine of prose-as-architecture. Author of *Madame Bovary* (1857), prosecuted for obscenity in the same year as Baudelaire's *Les Fleurs du Mal*. His correspondence — vast and theoretically rich — is as important to his literary legacy as the fiction.

## Major Works

- *Madame Bovary* (1857) — not reviewed in this wiki
- *L'Éducation sentimentale* (1869) — not reviewed in this wiki
- [*Correspondencia Flaubert-Baudelaire*](../works/flaubert-baudelaire_correspondencia.md) — letters; reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Prose-as-architecture: the doctrine of *le mot juste*, impersonality, the labor of the sentence. In the letters, a candor and ferocity absent from the novels — Flaubert the correspondent is less controlled, more polemical, funnier than Flaubert the novelist.

## Critical Reputation

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): the correspondence with Baudelaire illuminates both writers through contrast — Flaubert's impersonality vs. Baudelaire's subjectivity, prose-as-architecture vs. prose-as-intoxication.
