# Walter Benjamin

## Biographical Context

Born 1892 in Berlin; died 1940 in Portbou, Catalonia, Spain, by his own hand while fleeing the Nazis. One of the most influential critical theorists and essayists of the 20th century, associated with the Frankfurt School. His major works include "La obra de arte en la época de su reproductibilidad técnica," *Das Passagen-Werk* (Arcades Project), and "El narrador." His work was largely unpublished or unacknowledged in his lifetime; posthumous reception has established him as a canonical figure in critical theory and literary studies. ## Style and Technique

Per [Gonzalo Torné's review of *Radio Benjamin*](../sources/2025-10-01_radio-benjamin.md): In the radio broadcasts, Benjamin simplifies syntax and abandons erudite exquisiteness, but never adapts topics to an Adamic vision of childhood or hastens toward moral conclusions. He treats children as "a less trained species of adults who deserve respect." Even in this accessible register, he filters reflections on moral questions "interwoven with politics and social dynamics." Torné reads this as the storyteller's art — in practice — that Benjamin theorised in his essay on the narrator.

Torné identifies Benjamin's intellectual tics: "reticence to linear argumentation, distrust of conclusions, taste for the aphorism, the parable, and the enigmatic fulguration of an intelligence very close and somewhat strange — closer the stranger it is."
