# Sara Barquinero

## Biographical Context

Sara Barquinero is a Spanish novelist whose debut *Los escorpiones* (2024) attracted wide critical attention. She is associated with the conceptual and ambiguous tradition of literary experimentation.

## Major Works

- *Los escorpiones* (2024) — debut; mentioned in Mora's review but not directly reviewed in this wiki
- [*La chica más lista que conozco* (2026)](../works/barquinero_chica-mas-lista.md) — reviewed by Vicente Luis Mora

## Style and Technique

Structural ambition drawn from philosophy: *La chica más lista* is modeled on the Kantian treatise (chapters in redonda + italicized observations). A dual narrative register: the exposition of Alicia's university years as *Bildungsroman*, and omniscient author-voice observations in the manner of Galdós/Pardo Bazán — but without their paternalism, delivered with greater velocity and critical sharpness. When Alicia's rational project fails (pp. 347–351), the formal structure breaks — fragmentation, loss of numerical ordering — the form performing the content. David Foster Wallace is an acknowledged influence; her work belongs to the "novelas de la teoría" tradition of the 21st century.

## Critical Reputation

[Vicente Luis Mora](../critics/mora_vicente-luis.md) considers her one of the most interesting current Spanish narrative voices. He rates *La chica más lista* as technically superior to *Los escorpiones*, if less ambitious in scope.
