# Laure Murat

## Biographical Context

Born 1967 in Neuilly-sur-Seine, France. Historian and writer; professor at UCLA. Repudiated daughter of an aristocratic family whose surnames appear in Proust's *À la recherche du temps perdu*. Her family owns a castle that features in *Proust, novela familiar*. Her vital decisions — working, being feminist, loving another woman — made her "unassumable" for her family. ## Style and Technique

Per [Gonzalo Torné's review of *Proust, novela familiar*](../sources/2025-05-12_proust-novela-familiar-murat.md): Murat's tone is one of familiarity — she brings the emotional containment she learned from her aristocratic family but adds "touches of deep understanding and tenderness that give the measure of the woman she has become by her own merits." The book navigates the danger of replicating aristocratic emptiness (displays of names and dates) through vivid portraits and precise prose. The absence of reproach and drama in narrating her family exclusion is its most striking quality.
