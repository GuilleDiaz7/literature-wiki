# Andre Dubus

## Biographical Context

American short story writer. Born Lake Charles, Louisiana (1936); died Haverhill, Massachusetts (1999). Works in the tradition of American working-class short fiction associated with Carver, Cheever, and Hemingway.

## Major Works

- [*Encontrar a una chica en América*](../works/dubus_encontrar-chica.md) — stories and a novella; reviewed by Torné 2024

## Style and Technique

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): Dubus fits standard US short fiction templates — hyper-realism, psychological observations that respect the reader's moral sensibility, colourless descriptions. Model-dependence is visible: Conrad, Lawrence, Hemingway surface openly. Competent but limited; the title novella (about a middle-aged professor-writer and his successive loves) lacks the psychological deepening a longer piece demands.

## Critical Reputation

[Torné, 2024](../sources/2024-04-03_encontrar-chica-dubus.md): a reliable practitioner in a tradition he does not renew. Torné's broader argument is that the "American mine" of working-class minimalism has been largely exhausted; Dubus is cited as evidence rather than exception. Torné notes that Chekhov's name always surfaces to legitimize US short fiction, though none of these authors actually resemble Chekhov.
