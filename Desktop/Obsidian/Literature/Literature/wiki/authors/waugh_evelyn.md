# Evelyn Waugh

## Biographical Context

Born 1903 in West Hampstead, London; died 1966 in Combe Florey, Somerset. Satirical novelist of the English upper classes. Known for his Catholic conversion (1930) and for a series of comic and satirical novels (*Decline and Fall*, *Vile Bodies*, *Scoop*, *Brideshead Revisited*, the Sword of Honour trilogy). ## Style and Technique

Per [Gonzalo Torné's review of *Un puñado de polvo*](../sources/2025-06-04_punado-polvo-waugh.md): Waugh's writing in this novel is "decisively refractory to the literary" — bare dialogue, no transitions, no interiority, no ornament. The result resembles the skeleton of a television script. Torné distinguishes this from Waugh's admired Dickens model: Waugh deliberately sacrifices Dickensian prose juiciness for speed and moral repugnance. The style is purposive, achieving forensic detachment, the transmission of moral repugnance, aristocratic cynicism, and a tone mixing humour with bitterness.
