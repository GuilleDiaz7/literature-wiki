# Michael Powell

## Biographical Context

British film director, best known for the Powell-Pressburger partnership (The Archers, 1941–1959), which produced *The Red Shoes*, *A Matter of Life and Death*, *Black Narcissus*, and others. The received understanding of the partnership — Pressburger as writer, Powell as visual craftsman — is contested by [Miguel Marías](../critics/marias_miguel.md), who argues the division of labour was not that clean. Powell wrote two volumes of memoirs late in life (*A Life in Movies*, 1986; *Million-Dollar Movie*, 1992) and one novel, *A Waiting Game* (1975), which he never mentioned in either memoir.

## Critical Reputation

[Miguel Marías](../critics/marias_miguel.md) rates *A Waiting Game* as high as Powell's greatest films. Marías's perspective is that of a film critic, not a literary scholar; his assessment situates Powell's novel within a film career rather than a literary tradition.
