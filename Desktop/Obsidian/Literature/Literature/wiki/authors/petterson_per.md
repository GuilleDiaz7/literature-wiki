# Per Petterson

## Biographical Context

Per Petterson (Oslo, Norway, 1952) is a Norwegian novelist. His career was marked by a personal tragedy: the death of his parents and brother in the *Scandinavian Star* ferry disaster (1990).

## Major Works

- [*Salir a robar caballos* (*Ut og stjæle hester*, 2003)](../works/petterson_salir-robar-caballos.md) — IMPAC Dublin Literary Award; reviewed by Torné
- *No me robes* (*Jeg forbanner tidens elv*, 2008) — mentioned; not reviewed
- Others in the same ongoing project — not reviewed

## Style and Technique

Petterson's novels function as both independent works and chapters in an evolving single book, revising and extending the exposure of his characters' lives from multiple temporal and social angles. His evident strengths: description of natural landscapes, construction of mundane voices, everyday emotionality. Subtler strengths: the contrast between muted present and vivid past palettes; very audacious ellipses. His defining contribution: time as geometric form — not a continuous flow that never returns, but a shape allowing one to see events from different positions.

## Critical Reputation

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) makes an unusually strong comparative claim: Petterson is one of the very few active novelists with a personal and achieved literary project, well above Houellebecq, Cărtărescu, and Franzen in ambition and talent.
