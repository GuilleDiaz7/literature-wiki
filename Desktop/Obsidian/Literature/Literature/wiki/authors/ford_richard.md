# Richard Ford

## Biographical Context

Richard Ford (Jackson, Mississippi, 1944) is an American novelist associated with the tradition of literary realism. He won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction for *Independence Day* (1995), the second Frank Bascombe novel. He has received honorary doctorates and the Princess of Asturias Award for Literature (2016).

## Major Works

- *The Sportswriter* (1986) — first Frank Bascombe novel; not reviewed in this wiki
- *Independence Day* (1995) — Pulitzer Prize; not reviewed
- *The Lay of the Land* (2006) — not reviewed
- *Let Me Be Frank With You* (2014) — not reviewed
- [*Sé mía* (*Be Mine*, 2023)](../works/ford_se-mia.md) — fifth Frank Bascombe novel; reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Atone prose; naming everything (brands, roads, tires, stores) — a curiously American mania Torné compares to Updike's sensual delirium but without its erotic charge. Cold stoicism as dominant mode — disillusioned lucidity without remorse. This tone makes the rare crest of emotivity (a late-love scene with a young masseuse) all the more penetrating. The Bascombe saga is governed by the quintessential American question: how do we assess happiness under the paradigm of self-improvement?

## Critical Reputation

Ford is a respected but not universally celebrated figure in American letters. [Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md)'s review is appreciative of the emotional architecture while noting the irony of Frank as a "sack of blows" who has suffered far more than the average man for the reader's entertainment.
