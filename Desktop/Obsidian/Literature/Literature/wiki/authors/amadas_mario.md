# Mario Amadas

## Biographical Context

Mario Amadas (Barcelona, 1986) is a Spanish writer working in small-press contexts. His literary adventure has so far circulated in "diminutive print runs" — a situation Torné attributes to editorial blindness rather than lack of quality.

## Major Works

- *El día que pase algo* (Colectivo La Máquina, 2021) — mentioned; not reviewed in this wiki
- *Diario de Brooklyn* (Ril Editores, 2019) — mentioned; not reviewed
- [*Las fechas exactas. Setenta días en Ghana*](../works/amadas_fechas-exactas.md) — reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Amadas's primary literary quality is his precision: both phenomenological (reconstructing sensations, climate, textures so vividly the description doesn't sound like description) and moral (a continuous scrutiny of surrounding human activity, hypersensitive to incompetence, disloyalty, laziness, and abuse). These qualities are crossed by good humor and rectitud bondadosa. Imprevisible (unpredictable even on well-worn topics).

## Critical Reputation

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) calls him one of the few "original protrusions" of otherwise predictable and compliant Spanish prose. Urges readers to find any of his works. Strong advocacy in a context of editorial invisibility.
