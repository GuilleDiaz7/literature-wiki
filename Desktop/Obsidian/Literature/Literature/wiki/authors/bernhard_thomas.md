# Thomas Bernhard

## Biographical Context

Born 1931 in Heerlen, Netherlands; died 1989 in Gmunden, Austria. One of the most significant Austrian writers of the 20th century, known for relentless critique of Austrian society, Catholicism, and cultural institutions. His will famously forbade publication or performance of his works in Austria for the duration of copyright. ## Style and Technique

Per [Gonzalo Torné's review of *Andar*](../sources/2025-06-28_andar-bernhard.md): Bernhard's prose is a "sadistic device" — an echo chamber where voices reflect others' words (often memories of a third party), sensitive to paranoid and delirious nuances applied to banal topics, absurd parallelisms, sudden pronouncements, and games with prepositions and italicised emphases that leave the reader "somewhat bewildered." This bewilderment is the intended effect, not a side effect.

Torné's broader context: Bernhard, like Borges, is nearly impossible to imitate without falling into parody. The "Bernhard virus" that infected Spanish writers in the 1990s is well documented. The one writer whose prose genuinely benefited was his most constant translator, [Miguel Sáenz](../translators/saenz_miguel.md).
