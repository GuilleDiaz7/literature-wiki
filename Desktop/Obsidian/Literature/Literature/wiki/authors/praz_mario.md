# Mario Praz

## Biographical Context

Mario Praz (Rome, 1896–1982) was an Italian literary critic and comparatist, author of the influential *La carne, la morte e il diavolo nella letteratura romantica* (1930). He travelled to Greece in 1931 and recorded the visit in *Viaje a Grecia*.

## Major Works

- *La carne, la muerte y el diablo en la literatura romántica* (1930) — major scholarly work; not reviewed in this wiki
- [*Viaje a Grecia* (1931)](../works/praz_viaje-grecia.md) — reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Erudition of a "Mozart" caliber: acutely sensitive to forms, colors, textures, birdsong, geographical accidents. A humanist for whom classical education constitutes a shared civilization. His travel writing combines irony (toward nouveau-riche Americans), compassion (toward the Greek poor), and painful lucidity about what Greeks have surrendered in exchange for exposure of their glories. His natural descriptions possess a precision and beauty worthy of the Romantic travel tradition he himself studied.

## Critical Reputation

Largely a scholarly reputation (the Gothic/Romantic literature study). [Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) focuses on the travel essay, judging Praz's nature descriptions worthy of an anthology of writers who loved Greece.
