# Jon Fosse

## Biographical Context

Jon Fosse (Haugesund, Norway, 1959) received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2023. He is primarily a dramatist — very well represented in France and extensively performed and awarded there — though he has also written prose fiction. In Spain, only a small portion of his extensive work has been translated.

## Major Works

- Extensive theatrical work — not reviewed in this wiki
- [*Mañana y tarde* (*Morgon og kveld*, 2000)](../works/fosse_manana-tarde.md) — reviewed by Torné

## Style and Technique

Repetition-based syntax; litanic quality that may recall [Thomas Bernhard](bernhard_thomas.md) (though Fosse is less concentrated and obsessive) or the enveloping salmodias of the Bible. Wordplay and verbal effects at the service of a "slightly disheveled poetry" — the language never quite dances. But the combination is highly sensitive to special states: the gaze of a new mother, the first hearings of a newborn, the weight of everyday tools charged with experience. Speaks of everyday mysteries without intellectual sophistication.

## Critical Reputation

The Nobel Prize has foregrounded Fosse internationally. [Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) cautions that *Mañana y tarde* is only a "finger dip into the sea" of Fosse's work, and cannot be taken as representative of an oeuvre primarily built in theater.
