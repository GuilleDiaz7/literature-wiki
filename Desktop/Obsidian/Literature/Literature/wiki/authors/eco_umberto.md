# Umberto Eco

## Biographical Context

Umberto Eco (Alessandria, 1932–Milan, 2016) was an internationally acclaimed writer, philosopher, medievalist, and semiotician. His fiction — including *El nombre de la rosa* (1980), *El péndulo de Foucault* (1988), and *El cementerio de Praga* (2010) — achieved worldwide success. He also wrote critical and theoretical works. He was a recipient of Italy's Premio Strega, the Princess of Asturias Award, and a Chevalier de la Légion d'Honneur.

## Major Works

- *El nombre de la rosa* (1980) — international bestseller; not reviewed in this wiki
- *El péndulo de Foucault* (1988) — not reviewed
- *On the Shoulders of Giants* (2019, posthumous) — source of the "Cult of the Imperfect" essay reviewed in this wiki
- Critical works: *The Open Work* (*Opera aperta*), *Six Walks in the Fictional Woods*, etc.

## Style and Technique

As a theorist: combines semiotic rigor with readability; uses extended examples drawn from popular culture (*Casablanca*, *Monte Cristo*) to make theoretical points accessible without losing precision. His prose has the quality of sustained, witty argument.

## Critical Reputation

Unusual in the wiki: Eco enters not as the subject of a review but as a critic/theorist whose essay ["The Cult of the Imperfect"](../sources/2019-10-28_cult-imperfect-eco.md) is the source. His theory of ramshackle cult objects is relevant to multiple entries in the wiki (particularly [El Mago](../works/fowles_el-mago.md) and the proposed concept page on "negative charisma").
