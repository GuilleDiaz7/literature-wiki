# Vicente Luis Mora

## Biographical Context

Vicente Luis Mora is a Spanish critic and novelist, associated with the study of contemporary Spanish narrative. He is one of the more theoretically engaged critics of his generation, attentive to form, structure, and the relationship between literary fiction and philosophy. His criticism tends toward close formal analysis rather than comparative ranking.

## Critical Method

Mora's approach is structurally focused: he tracks how novels build their architecture, how formal choices encode meaning, how the "novelas de la teoría" tradition (21st-century Spanish fiction influenced by DFW, Perec, and philosophical models) operates. He names and frames technical achievements precisely — e.g., the Kantian treatise structure of Barquinero's *La chica más lista*, the dual register of chapter types, the moment when form breaks to perform content (pp. 347–351).

He reads within a tradition: his praise for Barquinero situates her against 19th-century omniscient narrators (Galdós, Pardo Bazán) and 21st-century structural innovators (DFW). Unlike Torné, he does not construct explicit comparative hierarchies across national literatures.
