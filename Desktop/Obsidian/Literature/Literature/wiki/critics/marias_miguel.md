# Miguel Marías

## Biographical Context

Spanish critic and film scholar, known for his work on cinema as well as literature. His contribution to this wiki comes via a prologue to the Spanish edition of Anthony Powell's *Juego de espera* (*A Question of Upbringing*, first volume of *A Dance to the Music of Time*). Marías belongs to a generation of Spanish critics who championed underread 20th-century European novels during the period of cultural openness after the Franco era.

## Critical Method

Canon-building and recovery: Marías writes as an advocate for works he believes have been unjustly neglected or intimidated readers by their scope. His framing of Powell is comparative and contextualizing — setting Powell against Proust to establish the scale and ambition of the *Dance* sequence, then arguing for Powell's distinctiveness within that comparison.

His critical mode is generous and long-view: he sees a writer's achievement across a complete body of work and constructs the argument for why that achievement matters. Less interested in evaluative ranking of individual volumes than in establishing the case for serious engagement.
