# Matías Serra Bradford

## Biographical Context

Argentine critic, writes for *Clarín* (Buenos Aires). Brings a Latin American perspective to the wiki — the first Argentine-publication source in the collection. His attention to Julio Ramón Ribeyro reflects a Buenos Aires critical culture that has long championed Peruvian and Rioplatense diarist traditions.

## Critical Method

Serra Bradford's criticism is phenomenological and voice-attending: he focuses on what it feels like to read a given writer, what texture a prose produces, how a voice situates itself in time. Less interested in comparative hierarchies than in the specificity of the encounter between reader and text.

In his piece on Ribeyro, Serra Bradford foregrounds the diary over the stories — privileging the more intimate, less polished form as Ribeyro's deepest achievement. This represents a valuation inverse to much academic reception, which focuses on the stories. His reading is attentive to the psychological structure named in Ribeyro's title: "the temptation of failure" as a genuine psychic orientation, not merely a metaphor.
