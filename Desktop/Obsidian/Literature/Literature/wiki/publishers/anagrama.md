# Anagrama

## Editorial Profile

Founded in Barcelona in 1968 by Jorge Herralde, described by Gil de Biedma as "ingeniero con incurable debilidad por las letras." The first books appeared at the Día del Libro 1969; the first title in print was *L'ofici de viure* by Pavese (Catalan collection). [Bonilla, 2022](../sources/2022-04-16_herralde-bonilla.md)

Bonilla's assessment: "desde 1968 al presente, nadie en España ha creado tantos nuevos lectores como Jorge Herralde." The editorial mission, as he frames it, is to produce *el lector exento* — the reader who is only a reader, not simultaneously a journalist, author, or critic. [Bonilla, 2022](../sources/2022-04-16_herralde-bonilla.md)

**Phases of the catalog** (per Bonilla):
- *Late 1960s–early 1970s*: political essay and Catalan titles; Cuadernos Anagrama
- *Mid-1970s*: Informal collection — Vázquez Montalbán, Tom Wolfe, Donald Barthelme, Shakespeare's sonnets (trans. Agustín García Calvo)
- *Crisis*: bankruptcy of Enlace (distributor) forced a pivot toward narrative fiction
- *From ~1980*: **Panorama de Narrativas** (yellow collection) — modeled on Gallimard's *Du monde entier*; became the defining collection. First major success: *La conjura de los necios* (Toole, acquired for $1,000 from Louisiana University Press)
- *1986*: **Biblioteca Nabokov** — *Pnin*, *Habla, memoria*, *Ada o el ardor*, *Lolita*, *El ojo*

**Uniform covers as identity**: Herralde identified early that consistent design was essential to reader trust — buyers would acquire an unknown author purely on the strength of the imprint. [Bonilla, 2022](../sources/2022-04-16_herralde-bonilla.md)

**Herralde's editorial character**: combative in defending authors, not hesitant to challenge critics who gave poor reviews or editors who failed to cover his books. His rejection letters to authors were equally direct. [Bonilla, 2022](../sources/2022-04-16_herralde-bonilla.md)

## Thematic Trends

Based on the 5 works in this wiki:

- **Contemporary anglophone fiction dominates** — 3 of 5 works are English-language novels (Ford, Fowles, Nunez), consistent with the Panorama de Narrativas tradition
- **Essay and memoir** — Murat's Proust essay fits Anagrama's long essay tradition (Argumentos, Cuadernos)
- **Female creation and autonomy** — Tokarczuk's feminist parody; Nunez's vulnerability; Murat's self-liberation through Proust
- **Emotional interiority** — Ford's late-style mortality, Nunez's fragile connections, Murat's aristocratic self-examination
- **Languages**: English (3), French (1), Polish (1) — all in Spanish translation
