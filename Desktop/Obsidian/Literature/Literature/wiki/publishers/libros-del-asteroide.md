# Libros del Asteroide

## Editorial Profile

Independent Barcelona publisher founded in 2005. Specialises in contemporary international fiction — principally anglophone — with a strong preference for literary seriousness over commercial formula. Known for elegant design and a coherent aesthetic identity. Consistently strong in contemporary British and Irish fiction. [unsourced — verify: general knowledge]

## Thematic Trends

Based on the 4 works in this wiki:

- **Almost exclusively anglophone** — 3 of 4 works originally in English (Cusk, Galgut, Hope); only Petterson is an exception (Norwegian)
- **Female creation and authorship** — Cusk (*Desfile*) and Hope (*Albión*) both engage with women's creative autonomy and social constraint; creación femenina is a shared thread
- **Travel, displacement, dislocation** — Galgut's three travel novellas; Petterson's Norway as emotional landscape; Hope's Sussex estate as site of colonial revelation: place as unsettling force
- **Contemporary and recent fiction** — 3 of 4 works are from 2003 onward; Galgut (2006) is the oldest. Clear focus on living or recently deceased authors
- **Quiet formalism** — Cusk's autofictional register, Petterson's temporal geometry, Galgut's uneven novella structure: all resist plot-driven narrative
- **Languages**: English (3), Norwegian (1)
