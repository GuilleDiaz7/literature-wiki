# Impedimenta

## Editorial Profile

Independent Madrid publisher with a sharp identity: canonical works previously unavailable or poorly served in Spanish translation, alongside contemporary literature from Eastern Europe and Northern Europe. Known for careful production, readable translations, and a consistent taste for the oblique, the formally strange, and the historically eccentric. [unsourced — verify: general knowledge]

## Thematic Trends

Based on the 4 works in this wiki:

- **Eastern European literature** — Gospodínov (Bulgarian, 2 works) and Starobinets (Russian) make up 3 of 4 entries. A clear editorial commitment to post-Soviet and Eastern European voices
- **The fantastic and the oblique** — Starobinets's fox-women and mad scientists; Gospodínov's anti-linear orality and Minotaur empathy; Waugh's bitter social comedy. None are straightforwardly realist
- **Historical violence and its aftermath** — Starobinets (WWII China), Gospodínov (Bulgarian historical memory), Waugh (interwar British decline): history as pressure rather than backdrop
- **Formal experimentation** — short fiction, anti-linear structure, simulated orality (Gospodínov); eccentric historicism (Starobinets); bare satirical style (Waugh)
- **Canonical recovery** — Waugh (1934) sits alongside contemporary works; Impedimenta consistently rescues under-translated classics
- **Languages**: Bulgarian (2), Russian (1), English (1)
