# Source: Torné, "Italo Calvino, el autor que escribía subido a un árbol" (2023)

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "Italo Calvino, el autor que escribía subido a un árbol." *El Periódico*, 7 September 2023. (Calvino centenary: born 15 October 1923.)

No single reviewed work — this is a centenary critical essay covering Calvino's career.

## Summary

Torné surveys Calvino's career across several phases. (1) The early period: though there are indicators of a realist Calvino (*El sendero de los nidos de araña*, *Los jóvenes del Po*), he did not become himself until he "climbed the trees" — the fantasy trilogy *Nuestros antepasados*. The trilogy starts from fantastic situations (a halved viscount; a hollow armour animated by pure will) readable as symbolic, but Calvino's territory is far from Kafka's suggestive opacity. The central novel, *El barón rampante*, is the best — not quite fantasy but "inverosímil": an adolescent who refuses to descend from the treetops, which is less a state change than a perspective shift. Calvino avoids symbolism of withdrawal, deploying narrative possibilities: how does one sleep, eat, love, relieve oneself among branches? He knows each wood's texture and how light illuminates foliage. "The best 18th-century French novel, written in the 20th century." (2) The return to realism in the early 1960s: *La nube de smog* integrates the fantasy trilogy's achievements into a verisimilar landscape — the protagonist's pathological hand-washing, urban contamination, nuclear fear scale up every 10 pages, while the grey epic of the office worker accumulates, held together by a symbolic dimension of smog. (3) The metamorphic phase: *El castillo de los destinos cruzados*, *Las ciudades invisibles* — formal experiments. *Las ciudades invisibles* risks being classified as "novela de cuentos" but tends toward lyric stamp over narration. Marco Polo's invented cities follow a Borgesian logic (a civilization's full character deducible from its classification system), but Calvino's organizing ideas are less penetrating than Borges's. (4) *Si una noche de invierno un viajero*: in its time seemingly opening new paths; now sounds somewhat worn — a festival of metafictional wit alternating relato with characters' commentary. The idea's audacity exceeds its page-by-page readability. Calvino's critical and essay writings (*De fábula*, *Seis propuestas*, *Por qué leer a los clásicos*, *Los libros de los otros*) are praised as a natural extension of his fiction's curious and playful intelligence. The defensive texts Calvino wrote about his own fiction are identified as products of factional pressure in a now-closed era — narrow, almost unrecognizable in tone.

## Key Claims

- Calvino found himself only in the fantasy trilogy; the early realism and the late metafiction are lesser achievements.
- *El barón rampante*: the best 18th-century French novel, written in the 20th century — the key evaluation.
- *Las ciudades invisibles*: Borgesian logic but less penetrating; lyric more than narrative.
- *Si una noche de invierno un viajero*: metafictional audacity outpaces its readability.
- Calvino's critical prose mirrors his fiction's intelligence.
- The defensive autoexegetical texts are relics of a now-closed era of poetic factional pressure.
