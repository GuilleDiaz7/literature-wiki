# Source: Torné, "Las Cartas de Lovecraft (I)" (2023)

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "Crítica de las 'Cartas' de Lovecraft: el taller de un artista concienzudo." *El Periódico*, 26 March 2023.

Reviewed work: H. P. Lovecraft, *Escribir contra los hombres. Cartas (I)*. Trans. Javier Calvo. Aristas Martínez Ediciones, 544 pp. 34,90 €.

## Summary

Torné approaches the letters from a position of admitted ignorance: he had never read Lovecraft and had no plans to do so, having found Gothic literary horror — including Poe, Lovecraft's acknowledged master — cold and boring. His prejudices expected an intuitive, dark, somewhat sloppy author with occult beliefs. Instead the letters reveal a conscientious, rational, conscious, and laborious artist. Lovecraft hated sentimental, cheap, and popular art; he spent his life extracting, very slowly from his own sensibility, stories dedicated to "lo extraño" — the representation of pressures the unknown exerts upon human nerves. He considered the sensation of strangeness a primary experience of consciousness, comparable to falling in love, ambition, or war. He also believed that as science eroded old fears (witches, ghosts, demons), strangeness would migrate to the immensities of time and the dark reaches of space — his work bridges Gothic terror and science fiction. The greatest spectacle the letters offer is Lovecraft's precision and insistence in defining "the strange" and distinguishing artistic from merely popular horror. Editor Javier Calvo's prologue and annual biographical introductions constitute a sentient and erudite biography of Lovecraft. A second volume on dreams and a third on political ideas are promised.

## Key Claims

- Lovecraft is revealed as conscientious, rational, laborious — the opposite of the intuitive occultist Torné expected.
- His concept of "lo extraño" is a primary category of consciousness, not genre entertainment.
- He bridges Gothic terror and science fiction historically.
- His letters are heroic given that his work was published in low-prestige magazines.
- Javier Calvo's edition (prologue + annual biography) is praised as erudite and felt.
- Translator/editor: Javier Calvo; publisher: Aristas Martínez Ediciones.
