# Source: Bonilla, "Herralde" (2022)

## Bibliographic Details

Bonilla, Juan. "Herralde." *Jot Down*, 16 April 2022. https://www.jotdown.es/2022/04/herralde/

Written on the occasion of the publication of *Los papeles de Herralde*, ed. Jordi Gracia. Essay combining personal memoir with analysis of the epistolary volume.

## Summary

Bonilla — who identifies himself as a reader shaped by Anagrama but not one of its published authors — writes a personal-critical essay about Jorge Herralde and the editorial history of Anagrama, anchored in the publication of *Los papeles de Herralde* (ed. Jordi Gracia), an edited volume of Herralde's correspondence. The essay moves between close reading of the letters and Bonilla's own biographical experience as a reader constructed by the Anagrama catalog.

Bonilla's central claim: "desde 1968 al presente, nadie en España ha creado tantos nuevos lectores como Jorge Herralde." The editorial mission, as Bonilla frames it, is not commercial but civilizational: to produce *el lector exento* — the reader who is only a reader, not simultaneously a journalist, author, professor, or critic.

The essay traces Anagrama's phases: the political essay period (late 1960s–early 1970s); the Cuadernos Anagrama; the Informal collection (mid-1970s: Wolfe, Barthelme, Vázquez Montalbán); the crisis following the bankruptcy of Enlace, their distributor; and the definitive consolidation of Panorama de Narrativas (the yellow collection, from ~1980), modeled consciously on Gallimard's *Du monde entier*. The Biblioteca Nabokov (1986) is noted as a defining moment.

A key editorial insight: Herralde identified uniform covers as essential to brand trust — readers would buy an unknown author purely because of the Anagrama imprint. Bonilla confirms this from personal experience.

The letters reveal Herralde's combativeness: he does not hesitate to write to critics who gave poor reviews, to editors-in-chief who failed to cover his authors, or to authors whose work disappointed him ("Has puesto tu testarudez, tan eficaz en otros casos, al servicio de una novela que está muy por debajo de tu previsible talento").

## Key Claims

- Anagrama founded 1968; first books appeared Día del Libro 1969.
- First book in print: *L'ofici de viure* by Pavese (Catalan collection).
- Herralde described by Gil de Biedma as "ingeniero con incurable debilidad por las letras."
- The yellow Panorama de Narrativas collection was modeled on Gallimard's *Du monde entier*.
- Uniform cover design was a deliberate identity strategy, not an aesthetic accident.
- Herralde personally defended his authors in correspondence with critics and editors.
- Key authors in the Anagrama canon per Bonilla: Bukowski, Carver, Amis, Carrère, Barnes, Pombo, Highsmith, Wilcock, Manganelli, Javier Marías, Nabokov, Bolaño, Toole (*La conjura de los necios*).
- The "American mine" of working-class minimalism (Carver tradition) was an early Anagrama pillar.
- Central editorial mission: creating *el lector exento*.

## Quotations of Significance

> "A nadie se le escapa que Herralde, el mítico Herralde a estas alturas, fundó la editorial más importante de las últimas décadas."

> "desde 1968 al presente, nadie en España ha creado tantos nuevos lectores como Jorge Herralde."

> "Era imposible no pensar que quien estuviese editando aquellos libros te conocía y sabía exactamente lo que ibas buscando en los libros."

## Limitations or Biases

Bonilla is an admirer and, by his own account, a reader formed by Anagrama. The essay is explicitly grateful rather than critical. His account of the catalog's influence is impressionistic and generational rather than documented.
