# Source: Torné, "Flaubert y Baudelaire: breve correspondencia" (2023)

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "Flaubert y Baudelaire: breve correspondencia en las alturas." *El Periódico*, 4 June 2023.

Reviewed work: Gustave Flaubert / Charles Baudelaire, *No se parece usted a nadie*. Ed. and Trans. Ignacio Echevarría. Alpha Decay, 128 pp. 14 €.

## Summary

The Baudelaire–Flaubert correspondence is brief — two or three topics, focused on their work as writers. The stature of both figures and the quality of their books invite fantasizing about a more extended exchange. Torné praises the edition by Ignacio Echevarría, whose contextualizing (historical, vital, cultural) makes the letters expand before the reader's eyes like dehydrated food rehydrated. Central topics: originality; the public role of the writer; the "priesthood of art"; how to preserve Romanticism's nerve while stripping it of sentimental excess (both associate this with the best Victor Hugo). Central event: the double obscenity trial against *Les fleurs du mal* and *Madame Bovary* — survived with different outcomes. The trials reveal a period when Puritan censorship expressed itself through power (not social media jokes) and when art was detaching itself from the protection of social and religious elites to pursue a "discourse of its own" that nobody knew quite what to do with. This moment led to the avant-gardes before being absorbed by the market. Flaubert and Baudelaire shared the same social class and generation; despite obvious character differences, both subverted the aesthetic values of their class (bourgeoisie), grew increasingly comfortable in its moral preceptors, and never questioned its political preeminence. The edition also permits a "reading of absences" — imagining what Flaubert would have said about *Les fleurs du mal*, what Baudelaire would have written about *L'Éducation sentimentale*.

## Key Claims

- The correspondence is brief but illuminated by Echevarría's contextualizing edition.
- Central themes: originality, the writer's public role, art's priesthood, the limits of sentimentalism.
- The obscenity trials mark the moment art detaches from aristocratic/religious patronage.
- Both writers subverted bourgeois aesthetics while respecting bourgeois morality and politics.
- Ignacio Echevarría is credited as translator/editor; publisher: Alpha Decay.
