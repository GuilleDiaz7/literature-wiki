# Source: Serra Bradford, "Julio Ramón Ribeyro" (2026)

## Bibliographic Details

Serra Bradford, Matías. "Julio Ramón Ribeyro y la gran revancha de las derrotas." *Clarín — Revista Ñ*, 3 April 2026.

Reviewed works: Julio Ramón Ribeyro, *La tentación del fracaso* (Seix Barral, 704 pp.); Julio Ramón Ribeyro, *La palabra del mudo* (Seix Barral). [Occasion: both reedited for Peru's invitation to the Buenos Aires Book Fair 2026.]

## Summary

Serra Bradford writes on the occasion of new Seix Barral reeditions of Ribeyro's diaries and complete stories. The essay focuses primarily on *La tentación del fracaso*, Ribeyro's diary (1950–1978), published as three volumes during his lifetime and later collected in one. Ribeyro (Lima, 1929–1994) was fundamentally solitary; the diary balanced that solitude by amplifying the importance of friendships. Serra Bradford surveys the diary's recurring concerns: moves and instability; insecurity; technical problems and procrastination; cyclothymic reading; the work done wrong; collaborations as competitive mirrors; the combination of precariousness and extravagance; health occlusion; the passage of time (even at 30 Ribeyro felt the surplus of his past accumulating). The diary connects to the short story collection (*La palabra del mudo*) through shared themes — the assessment of whether someone's potential was fully realized, the ascent of cultural prestige or positions, what traces a person leaves. *Prosas apátridas*, an inclassifiable collection of reflections and anecdotes, is identified as possibly Ribeyro's best self. Serra Bradford traces Ribeyro's literary lineage: Kafka, Woolf, Jünger (all studied by the Peruvian). He frames Ribeyro's diary as a genre phantom — the mold for a true work that was not (quite) realized, offering the pleasures of discontinuity and calendar echo. The essay ends on the image of the diarist and reader crossing on a staircase at odd hours: too shy to greet each other; one having told very personal things, the other going to spy on them.

## Key Claims

- *La tentación del fracaso* is the diary as "silicio" — self-mortification, a genre phantom for the true work.
- Ribeyro's genius is in *Prosas apátridas* and in the short stories (*La palabra del mudo*).
- The diary reveals: precariousness + extravagance; solitude + friendship; ambitious failure + heroic continuation.
- Ribeyro shares a Parisian exile lineage with César Vallejo and César Moro.
- Second critic in the wiki (after Mora): Matías Serra Bradford (Argentine, *Clarín*).
