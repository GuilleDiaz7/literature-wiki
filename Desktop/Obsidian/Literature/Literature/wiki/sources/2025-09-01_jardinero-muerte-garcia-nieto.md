# Source: García Nieto, "La botánica de la tristeza" (2025)

## Bibliographic Details

García Nieto, Rebeca. "La botánica de la tristeza de Gospodínov." *República de las Letras. Asociación Colegial de Escritores*, 1 September 2025.

Reviewed work: Gueorgui Gospodínov, *El jardinero y la muerte* (*Botanikata na skarbta*). Trans. María Vútova. Impedimenta, Madrid, May 2024, 224 pp. 22,95 €.

## Summary

García Nieto contextualizes the book within the now-substantial literary tradition of writing about the death of a father (Roth, Knausgård, Auster, Menéndez Salmón, Giralt Torrente, Fernández Mallo in Spain and elsewhere). She invokes Rulfo's claim that there are only three subjects: love, life, death. *El jardinero y la muerte* touches all three. The father, Dinyo Gospodínov, expressed himself primarily through his garden — his notebook contained only practical notes on pruning and irrigation, not existential confessions. The garden became central to the author's grieving. The father was the "living history of the family" — with him several worlds disappeared simultaneously. The book is characterized as hybrid (autobiographical material + essay-style reflection on language and memory): grief invades language (the word crucigrama becomes only a cross); writing can facilitate mourning by stripping painful associations from words. García Nieto identifies Gospodínov as a "humorista de la desesperación" — a phrase she says he inherited from his father, whose first words in the book ("Creo que me he hecho pis encima") embody this quality. She notes the book's tone: pesar (sorrow), tristeza (sadness), but also tenderness and elegance. Gospodínov neither avoids pain nor the most crude moments of the illness. Conclusion: after *Física de la tristeza* and *Las tempestálidas*, Gospodínov has written another great book.

## Key Claims

- *El jardinero y la muerte* belongs to the literary tradition of writing about the father's death.
- The father expressed himself through the garden; the garden became the site of mourning and memory.
- Grief invades language — writing works to cleanse that invasion.
- Gospodínov is a "humorista de la desesperación" — quality he inherited from his father.
- The book is hybrid: autobiography + essay on language and memory.
- Fourth critic in the wiki: Rebeca García Nieto (Spanish, *República de las Letras*).
- Translator: María Vútova; publisher: Impedimenta.
