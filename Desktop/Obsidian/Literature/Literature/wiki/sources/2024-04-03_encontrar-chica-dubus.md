# Source: Torné, "Encontrar a una chica en América, de Dubus" (2024)

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "'Encontrar a una chica en América', de Andre Dubus." *El Periódico*, 3 April 2024.

Reviewed work: Andre Dubus, *Encontrar a una chica en América*. Trans. David Paradela López. Gallo Negro, 192 pp. 19 €.

## Summary

Torné begins with a meditation on American narrative fiction as an inexhaustible mine of "precious metals" recovered from the past, noting that many of these authors share a recognizable poetics so internalized it has come to be identified with elegance and quality per se — while actually being merely a standard toolset. He invokes Chekhov (whose name always surfaces to legitimize US short fiction, though none of the authors actually resemble Chekhov), Carver, Cheever, Vonnegut. Dubus (Lake Charles, USA, 1936–Haverhill, USA, 1999) fits this template comfortably. Readers who love 19th-century British fiction will be comfortable here. But Torné's verdict is mixed: the pieces are miniatures without much élan, with dry dialogue and predictable endings, recalling their models too openly (Conrad for one story, Lawrence for another, Hemingway throughout). The title *nouvelle* (about Hank, a middle-aged professor-writer and his successive loves) exhibits Dubus's method fully: hyper-realism, psychological observations that respect the reader's moral sensibility, colourless descriptions. The narrator's emotional architecture does not deepen across the length of a novella. A reservation: four of this collection's stories were removed because Gallo Nero had already published them in a prior anthology (*Adulterio*) — whether those were better is unknown.

## Key Claims

- Dubus fits standard US short fiction templates: reliable, competent, limited.
- The collection is undermined by transparent model-dependence (Conrad, Lawrence, Hemingway).
- The title novella lacks the psychological growth a longer piece demands.
- The "gold mine of American narrative" has perhaps been exhausted.
- Translator: David Paradela López; publisher: Gallo Nero.
