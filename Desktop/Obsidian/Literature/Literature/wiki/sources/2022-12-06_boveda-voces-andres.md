# Source: Torné, "La bóveda y las voces, de Ramón Andrés" (2022)

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "'La bóveda y las voces', de Ramón Andrés: espacios de seguridad." *El Periódico*, 6 December 2022.

Reviewed work: Ramón Andrés, *La bóveda y las voces*. [Publisher not specified in source.]

## Summary

Torné begins with his general skepticism about genre-mixing books: they usually end in disaster, using each genre to cover the deficiencies of another, producing a Frankenstein with visible seams. Andrés escapes this pitfall because the genres assembled here (diary, biography, essay, investigation, music theory, cultural history) do not seem externally mixed but appear to emerge naturally from one another — the impression of a cultivated mind for which passing from a personal note to a reflection on polyphony, from a biographical sketch to a description of a path or tree, from a historical datum to a moral observation is entirely natural. Andrés reconstructs the life of Josquin Desprez, a 15th-century Flemish composer considered one of the great musicians of his time. His few biographically verifiable facts are expanded against the development of an original and complex music, itself connected to the arts of a century that seemed to be rehearsing spaces to slow down (or rest from) the anxious experience of time characteristic of the West (which Andrés considers a European creation). The book's formal audacity: it is an essay written as a diary, with the author's own voice (situated in Elizondo, Navarre, during a pandemic) moving between present and past, creating a simultaneity of temporal planes. Torné compares it to Sebald and Magris (a recognizable genre now). High praise for its essay on pandemic reclusión as analogous to the musical refuges Desprez composed.

## Key Claims

- The book succeeds as genre-hybrid because its elements emerge naturally from a cultivated mind, not artificially combined.
- Central theme: art as construction of immaterial spaces where time slows, becomes more human and substantial.
- Formal audacity: essay written as diary, voice of the author situated in his own present (pandemic).
- Compared to Sebald and Magris as a recognizable literary genre of cultured, twilight wandering.
- The pandemic reclusión is read as analogous to the musical "spaces of safety" Desprez built.

## Limitations or Biases

Torné is enthusiastic but does not examine whether the Sebald/Magris comparison accurately maps Andrés's book, which may be less peripatetic and more musically specific.
