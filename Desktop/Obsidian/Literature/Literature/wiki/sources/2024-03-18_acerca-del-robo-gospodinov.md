# Source: Torné, "Acerca del robo de historias, de Gospodínov" (2024)

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "'Acerca del robo de historias y otros relatos', de Gueorgui Gospodínov: noticias de la tradición oral." *El Periódico*, 18 March 2024.

Reviewed work: Gueorgui Gospodínov, *Acerca del robo de historias y otros relatos*. Trans. María Vútova. Impedimenta, 160 pp. 19,50 €.

## Summary

Torné opens by questioning the genre of this book — not poetry, drama, novel, chronicle, essay, or biography — and locating its interest precisely in its resistance to the norms of the literary *cuento*: Gospodínov rejects linear narration, psychological connective tissue, "plot twists," and morals. His stories pursue an oral breath, progressing through unexpected associations and highly original compositional angles (a country's history told through an ear; life with one eye in the past and one in the future). This audacity, Torné notes, is not unique to Gospodínov — comparable experiments appear in Eloy Tizón, Iban Zaldua, and Jordi Puntí. The book is brief, with numerous pieces, most succinct and unconcerned with atmosphere, description, or character development. Despite the apparent ease of the prose, Torné suspects each piece involves considerable labour, simulating a *oral tradition* from which stories descend into the book. Gospodínov's principal literary power here is the construction of witty, unexpected phrases and associations. The humour is never pure comedy: thick shadows of political critique and resigned resentment surface every few pages.

## Key Claims

- Gospodínov's *cuento* practice deliberately subverts the standard norms of literary short fiction: no linearity, no psychological tissue, no surprise endings.
- His stories simulate an oral tradition; each piece reads like a natural improvisation despite being carefully made.
- His primary literary power is the construction of *frases ingeniosas* with unexpected associations.
- Humour coexists with political critique and "resigned political resentment" — not merely comic.
- Occasional failures: the mainstream narrative current sometimes drags him to "familiar shores."
- Translator: María Vútova; publisher: Impedimenta.

## Relation to Existing Wiki

This is the second Gospodínov work reviewed by Torné in the wiki (after *Física de la tristeza*). In both cases Torné values Gospodínov's imaginative range but remains alert to failures of execution. The oral tradition / simulated improvisation theme here contrasts with the "field notebook" quality praised in *Física de la tristeza* — both descriptions point to the same anti-novelistic, vignette-based aesthetic.
