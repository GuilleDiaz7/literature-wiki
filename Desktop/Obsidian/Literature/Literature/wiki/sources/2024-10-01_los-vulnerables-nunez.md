# Source: "Los vulnerables, de Sigrid Nunez: los matices de la delicadeza"

URL: https://www.elperiodico.com/es/ocio-y-cultura/libros/20241001/sigrid-nunez-nuevo-libro-vulnerables-critica-117972230

Edition reviewed: Nunez, Sigrid. *Los vulnerables*. Trans. Mercedes Cebrián. Anagrama, 208 pp., €18.90.

## Summary

[Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) reviews Sigrid Nunez's *Los vulnerables*, a novel written under the sign of Covid-19 confinement. Torné characterises Nunez's style as schematic, allusive, learned, and analytical — prose more interested in presenting lives as summaries and examples of something than in showing them in expansive narration.

The novel opens with a series of crossed lives and memories of lost friendships, held in a tone Torné describes as "desenvolvuelto y delicado" — casual and delicate. Nunez punctuates this with quotations from prestigious authors, which she comments on with skill and combines with the narratives of lost friends to intensify their meaning. The narrative eventually settles into a concrete situation: an older woman (resembling the author) shares a small flat during lockdown with a young vegan she had never met before, and an imposing parrot. The opening's suggestive looseness projects over the whole, creating a sense of precariousness and fragility in relationships that can become very valuable if trust opens between the parties.

Torné places Nunez within a growing literary current that approaches the present as an open debate — not to expose its worst perspectives or acknowledge base passions, but to explore more delicate, unattended emotions: tenderness, trust, responsibility, care. He compares her project to Ali Smith's, though noting their approaches are distinct. Smith explores the potential of comprehending gestures to politically recover citizens lost to neoliberal hatred; Nunez opts for vulnerability, for the soft flanks of the human composite. For Torné, the analytical, schematic prose is the right stylistic choice for this purpose: it is concentrated on something smaller but more decisive — "los matices de la delicadeza" (the nuances of delicacy).

## Key Claims

- Nunez's style is deliberately schematic and analytical — a choice appropriate to her subject: the delicate, underattended registers of human emotion.
- The novel is positioned within a growing literary current exploring tenderness, vulnerability, and fragile connection rather than dramatic intensity and passion.
- Comparable to Ali Smith's project, though distinct: Nunez favours vulnerability over political recuperation.
- The novel's looseness at the opening, while suggestive, projects a sense of precariousness over the whole.
- Torné praises the novel's focus as "smaller but more decisive" — the nuances of delicacy.

## Quotations of Significance

> "Nunez se inclina por la vulnerabilidad, por los flancos suaves del compuesto humano, que se avienen mejor a su estilo, tan sensible a la ternura, a la empatía, a los repentinos brotes de afinidad, a las vacilaciones que se refuerzan en inesperadas alianzas."

> "Concentrada en algo, si se quiere, más pequeño, pero seguramente más decisivo: los matices de la delicadeza."

## Limitations or Biases

Torné's framework consistently privileges formal purposiveness — the idea that style must be justified by content. His praise of Nunez's schematic prose assumes this standard. Critics who value narrative density or immersive scene-building might read the same stylistic features as limitations rather than virtues.
