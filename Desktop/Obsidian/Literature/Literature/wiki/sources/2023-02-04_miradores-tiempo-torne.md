# Source: Torné, "Miradores del tiempo" (2023)

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "Miradores del tiempo." *El Periódico*, 4 February 2023.

Reviewed work: José Donoso, *Jane Austen y la elegancia del pensamiento*. Prologue by Cecilia García Huidobro Mc. Ediciones Lastarria & De Mora, 104 pp. 19 €.

## Summary

Torné reviews a recently republished undergraduate thesis by the Chilean novelist José Donoso (Santiago, 1924–1996) — his Princeton graduation essay, written at age 26, now edited with a scholarly prologue. Its central topic: the characters of Jane Austen, especially her female protagonists. The essay has a double appeal: as a walk through Austen's novels in good company, and as a glimpse into the workshop of interests of a major Latin American novelist in formation. Donoso begins with a respectable academic introduction to Romanticism, taste, and sentimentality, then moves to temperament analysis of the main Austen protagonists (Elizabeth Bennet, Emma Woodhouse, Anne Elliot, Fanny Price). Two "tópicos" about Austen are systematically dismantled. First: Nabokov's charge that Austen always wrote the same character — disproved by the contrast between Elizabeth and Emma, and by the radical difference represented by Anne Elliot. Second: the "mere intuition, especially female intuition" charge — disproved by the delicate construction behind each heroine, where natural talent (quick portraiture, steady eye for a scene) is combined with careful engineering. Torné closes: after reading Donoso on Austen, one cannot help returning to Austen's novels. And "where better could we be?"

## Key Claims

- Donoso's thesis dismantles two Austen clichés: (1) she always wrote the same character (refuted); (2) her technique is mere intuition (refuted).
- Anne Elliot is identified as a radical departure from Elizabeth and Emma.
- A young novelist's criticism is part autobiography, part prophecy.
- The edition: prologue by Cecilia García Huidobro Mc; Ediciones Lastarria & De Mora.
