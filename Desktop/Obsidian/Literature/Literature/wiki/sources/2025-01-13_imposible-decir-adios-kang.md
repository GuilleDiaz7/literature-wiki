# Source: "Imposible decir adiós, de Han Kang: lealtades y masacres"

## Bibliographic Details

Torné, Gonzalo. "'Imposible decir adiós', de Han Kang: lealtades y masacres." *El Periódico*, 13 January 2025.
URL: https://www.elperiodico.com/es/ocio-y-cultura/libros/20250113/han-kang-premio-nobel-nuevo-libro-117963075

Edition reviewed: Kang, Han. *Imposible decir adiós*. Trans. Sunme Yoon. Random House, 256 pp., €21.90.
[Note: Han Kang was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2024, the year before this review.]

## Summary

[Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) reviews Han Kang's *Imposible decir adiós*, characterising it as a two-faced coin: on its reverse, historical violence; on its obverse, that violence's effects on concrete living individuals. He resists summarising it quickly, preferring to slow down and examine its originality.

Torné identifies Kang's central formal strategy: historical violence — which he describes as more structural than political, "something that comes from far back in time, a river of blood and massacres, that seeks in each epoch a changing political justification" — is suppressed or repressed in the narrative, manifesting only through dreams, visions, blurred testimonies, and psychological and somatic pain. On the surface, the two protagonists (Gyeongha and Inseon) attempt to build a generous friendship at the limit of temerity. Torné frames the novel's question as: how to palliate historical, political, and social violence with a poetic attempt at good, assuming its difficulties and contradictions?

He then catalogues the novel's formal elements, each of which he describes as something a reader will notice in sequence. First, the precision with which Kang describes physical and mental pain. Second, the ease with which the prose moves between oneiric landscape and detailed, tactile realism, dissolving the border between them. Third, Kang's peculiar temporal syntax — spiralling backward and forward in time, where retrospective illuminations are as important as present-tense events, so that meaning seems to advance by retreating. Fourth, the plasticity with which Kang describes not nature itself but its structure, movement, and relation to bodies: rain, snowflakes, the interior of birds, the slow and malicious snowfall that symbolically seems intent on smothering the protagonists' remaining warmth.

Torné's verdict: the novel's atmosphere is dense, suggestive, almost hypnotic, and the story is "human, hard, tender, and sharpened by nervousness, on the edge of illness and redemption." He notes that here, unusually, formal sophistication intensifies rather than undermines emotion.

## Key Claims

- The novel is structured as a two-sided coin: suppressed historical violence (Korean massacres) on one side; its effects on living individuals on the other.
- Historical violence is formally repressed, appearing only through dreams, somatic symptoms, and blurred testimony — this is a deliberate structural choice, not evasion.
- Four formal achievements: (1) precision in describing physical/mental pain; (2) fluid movement between oneiric and tactile realism; (3) spiral temporal syntax; (4) plasticity in describing nature's structure and relation to bodies.
- The snowfall serves as the dominant symbolic/atmospheric element.
- Torné's central claim: sophistication here intensifies emotion rather than working against it.

## Quotations of Significance

> "Han Kang reprime esa violencia (que es más estructural que política: algo que viene de muy atrás del tiempo, un río de sangre y de masacres, que busca en cada época una justificación política cambiante) de manera que se manifiesta mediante sueños, visiones, testimonios borrosos y dolores psicológicos y somáticos."

> "La sofisticación aquí, como casi nunca, no juega en contra de la emoción sino que la intensifica y le da su razón de ser. La vuelve literaria."

## Limitations or Biases

Torné approaches *Imposible decir adiós* with unusual warmth relative to his other reviews in the wiki. His framework of formal assessment still operates — he is cataloguing and evaluating techniques — but his aesthetic verdict is unqualified in its praise. The political and historical context of Korean massacres (which the review does not spell out in historical detail) is treated as given; readers without knowledge of 20th-century Korean history may miss the specificity of the violence the novel addresses.
