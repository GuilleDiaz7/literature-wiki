# Source: Mora, "El tratado de Sara Barquinero" (2026)

## Bibliographic Details

Mora, Vicente Luis. "El tratado de Sara Barquinero." Blog post. vicenteluismora.blogspot.com, 5 April 2026.

Reviewed work: Sara Barquinero, *La chica más lista que conozco*. Barcelona: Lumen, 2026.

## Summary

This is the first source in the wiki by a critic other than Gonzalo Torné. Vicente Luis Mora, a Spanish critic and novelist, reviews Barquinero's second novel. His argument: *La chica más lista que conozco* is structurally modeled on the Kantian treatise — specifically the *Crítica de la razón pura* in the Pedro Ribas edition (Alfaguara, 1998). Chapters in redonda (exposition: Alicia's university years as a philosophy student in a capital city, a *Bildungsroman*) alternate with "Observaciones" in italics (numbered like notes or glosses), where an omniscient narrator voice intervenes in the manner of Galdós or Pardo Bazán — authorial opinions on power structures, class privilege, university hypocrisy — but without the paternalism Mora associates with those authors. The structure is not ironic but sincere: Barquinero uses the Kantian form as a vehicle to criticize the totality (the university institution) with the same means but contrary ends as her object of study. When the novel's logical sequence breaks — pages 347–351 — so does the formal structure (fragmentation, loss of numerical ordering), suggesting a solution that David Foster Wallace (Barquinero's acknowledged influence) would have appreciated. Mora situates the novel in the tradition of "novelas de la teoría" (theory novels) of the 21st century. He considers it a "better" novel than the celebrated *Los escorpiones* (2024) — more controlled, technically more achieved — though less *significant* (Barquinero's own word for what *Los escorpiones* attempted). He notes Barquinero as one of the most interesting current Spanish narrative voices.

## Key Claims

- Structural model: the Kantian treatise — exposition (chapters in redonda) + observations (italicized glosses).
- The critique of the university uses the form of Kantian philosophy against itself.
- The omniscient author-voice is compared to Galdós/Pardo Bazán but without their paternalism.
- The novel "breaks" (pp. 347–351) when Alicia's rational project crashes — the form performs the content.
- Mora considers it technically superior to *Los escorpiones* but less ambitious in scope.
- DFW influence; "novelas de la teoría" genealogy.
