# Mujer fatal / Homme fatal

## Definition

The *femme fatale* is a cultural and literary archetype: the woman whose sexuality or power destroys the men attracted to her. The archetype has a long history in 19th- and 20th-century literature and visual art (Salome, Carmen, Mata Hari). [Elisenda Julibert](../authors/julibert_elisenda.md)'s essay *Hombres fatales* argues that the archetype has operated as an ideological screen concealing an equally operative male equivalent — the *homme fatal* — whose dangerous power over others has been rendered culturally invisible.

The critical framework for understanding both archetypes combines:
- **René Girard**: mimetic desire — desire is not spontaneous but imitative, structured by rivalry and triangulation
- **Roland Barthes**: mythology as naturalization — the *femme fatale* is a cultural construction presented as natural, inevitable, biological
- **Clément Rosset**: reality and its double — the archetype operates as a doubling that displaces the real
