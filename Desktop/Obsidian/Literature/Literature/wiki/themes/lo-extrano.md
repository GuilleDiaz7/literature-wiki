# Lo extraño (The Weird)

## Definition

"Lo extraño" is the Spanish equivalent of what Anglophone criticism calls "the weird" — a literary mode distinct from Gothic horror, fantasy, or science fiction, though related to all three. The term is most clearly articulated in H.P. Lovecraft's essay "Supernatural Horror in Literature" (1927): the weird is characterized by a particular atmosphere of dread before the unknown, specifically before the intrusion of cosmic forces that are alien to human experience and scale.

Lovecraft's own fiction is the canonical instance, but the category extends backward (Poe, Machen, Blackwood) and forward (contemporary weird fiction). The weird is distinguished from Gothic horror by its emphasis on cosmic indifference rather than moral evil; from fantasy by its refusal of wonder or consolation; from science fiction by its insistence on the unknowable rather than the explicable.
