# Vulnerabilidad y delicadeza (Vulnerability and Delicacy)

## Definition

A cluster of emotional registers — tenderness, empathy, trust, responsibility, care, sudden affinity — that contemporary fiction is increasingly turning toward as its primary subject, in deliberate contrast to the dominant narrative appetite for impact, capture, and laceration. The theme encompasses both the fragility of human connection and the literary cultivation of attention to what is subtle and easily overlooked.

Within this wiki, the term is drawn primarily from [Gonzalo Torné's](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) reading of Sigrid Nunez: "the nuances of delicacy" as something "smaller but more decisive" than the emotional intensities favoured by mainstream narrative.

## Critical Treatments

- [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) situates Nunez within a growing literary current alongside Ali Smith. Where Smith explores comprehending gestures as political recuperation for citizens lost to neoliberal hatred, Nunez focuses on vulnerability itself — "the soft flanks of the human composite." Torné endorses this orientation as a literary choice: not smaller in importance, just smaller in volume.
