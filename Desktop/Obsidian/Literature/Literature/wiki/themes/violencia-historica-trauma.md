# Violencia histórica y trauma (Historical Violence and Trauma)

## Definition

The literary representation of collective historical violence — massacres, war, state terror — not as backdrop but as the buried structural substrate of present-day individual experience. In this thematic mode, historical violence is formally repressed in the narrative: it does not appear as documented event but resurfaces through dreams, somatic symptoms, blurred testimonies, psychological pain, and atmospheric disturbance.

The theme is distinguished from "war novel" or "historical fiction" by its focus on the *transmission* of violence across generations and its *embodied* manifestation in survivors and their descendants. The literary question is: how do you render structurally what resists direct narration?

Within this wiki, the concept is defined primarily through [Gonzalo Torné's](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) reading of Han Kang.

## Critical Treatments

- [Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) frames the structural repression of historical violence in Kang as a deliberate formal choice: the violence is "more structural than political — something that comes from far back in time, a river of blood and massacres, that seeks a different political justification in each epoch." Its repression into symptomatic form (dreams, somatic pain, blurred testimony) is the novel's defining formal operation.
