# El jardinero y la muerte

## Bibliographic Summary

By [Gueorgui Gospodínov](../authors/gospodinov_gueorgui.md). Published in Spanish by [publisher not specified in source]. Trans. María Vútova. Reviewed by [Rebeca García Nieto](../critics/garcia-nieto_rebeca.md) in September 2025. Second Gospodínov work reviewed in the wiki alongside *Física de la tristeza* and *Acerca del robo de historias*.

## Synopsis

A narrative in which a gardener and Death enter into some form of relationship or negotiation. The gardener tends a garden; Death is a figure who visits. The novel is characteristic Gospodínov in its use of the fable structure to approach existential themes obliquely — the absurdist or mythological frame enabling a treatment of mortality that direct realism would falsify.

## Critical Overview

[Rebeca García Nieto](../critics/garcia-nieto_rebeca.md): brings a different critical sensibility from Torné — less focused on comparative hierarchies, more attentive to the phenomenological and ethical dimensions of the text. The gardener-death relationship is read as an allegory of the writing life: the gardener tends what grows and must submit it to mortality. García Nieto notes María Vútova's translation work as a constant quality in Gospodínov's Spanish reception.
