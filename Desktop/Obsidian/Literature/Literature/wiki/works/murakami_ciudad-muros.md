# La ciudad y sus muros inciertos

## Bibliographic Summary

*Machi to sono futashika na kabe* (2023). Murakami's most recent novel. Reviewed by Torné in April 2024. Expands on an early story (1980) revisited and extended.

## Synopsis

A man enters a walled city that may be an inner psychic landscape. He must leave his shadow behind at the gate. Inside the city he encounters a woman and works as a librarian reading old dreams. The novel returns to the liminal, dreamlike geography Murakami established in earlier work, particularly *Hard-Boiled Wonderland and the End of the World* (1985), of which this is a spiritual successor.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): Murakami operates as a formula novelist whose mechanisms are well established and reliably produce a certain effect — but no more. His is an intermediate space: above Isabel Allende but below Gabriel García Márquez in the magical realism hierarchy.

> [!contradiction]
> Torné's assessment of Murakami as a "formula novelist" sitting below García Márquez contradicts the general critical reception, which tends to treat Murakami as a more sui generis figure not directly comparable to the García Márquez tradition. Torné's hierarchy (Allende < Murakami < García Márquez) is a personal critical position, not a consensus view.

## Interpretive Controversies

### Controversy: Murakami's Place in Magical Realism

- **Position A** (Torné): Murakami is a capable but limited formula novelist occupying a middle position in a hierarchy of magical realist writers.
- **Position B** (implied general reception): Murakami's work draws on distinct Japanese genre traditions (literary bundan fiction, urban fantasy) and is not best understood through the magical realism framework derived from Latin American literature.
- **Status**: Single critical voice in this wiki.
