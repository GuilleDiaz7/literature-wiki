# La chica más lista que conozco

## Bibliographic Summary

Second novel by [Sara Barquinero](../authors/barquinero_sara.md), following *Los escorpiones* (2024). Reviewed by [Vicente Luis Mora](../critics/mora_vicente-luis.md) in April 2026.

## Synopsis

Alicia, a young woman at university, attempts to construct her life according to a rational system — a philosophical project in the manner of Kant's critical treatise. The novel is structured accordingly: chapters in *redonda* narrate her Bildungsroman, while chapters in italics offer omniscient author-voice observations in the manner of 19th-century narrators (Galdós, Pardo Bazán), though without their paternalism, delivered at greater velocity and with more critical sharpness. When Alicia's rational system collapses (pp. 347–351), the formal structure breaks with it: fragmentation, loss of chapter numbering, form performing content.

## Critical Overview

[Vicente Luis Mora](../critics/mora_vicente-luis.md): the Kantian structure is not decorative — it is the load-bearing architecture of the novel. The dual narrative register (realist narrative + omniscient commentary) belongs to the "novelas de la teoría" tradition of 21st-century Spanish fiction. David Foster Wallace is an acknowledged influence. Mora rates this novel as technically superior to *Los escorpiones*, if less ambitious in scope. He considers Barquinero one of the most interesting voices in current Spanish narrative.
