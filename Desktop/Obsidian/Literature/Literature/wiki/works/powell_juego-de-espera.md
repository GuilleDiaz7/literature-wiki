# Juego de espera

## Bibliographic Summary

*A Waiting Game* (1975). Michael Powell's only novel, unmentioned in either of his two memoir volumes (*A Life in Movies*, 1986; *Million-Dollar Movie*, 1992). Spanish edition: *Juego de espera*. Trans. not specified. Reino de Redonda (prologue by [Miguel Marías](../critics/marias_miguel.md), published November 2019). Source: Marías's prologue republished as a blog post, April 2026.

## Synopsis

Ireland, 1952. The Donohue family — farmers and hunters — carry the weight of civil war memory and its lasting consequences for the present. The patriarchal figure voices this memory with chilling nostalgia: "the only real war is civil war... between neighbors and citizens of the same country... friend against friend, brother against brother." The novel is narratively well-constructed and full of surprises; [Marías, 2026](../sources/2026-04-13_powell-novela-marias.md) argues its narrative information structure cannot be translated directly to the screen — it is not a screenplay in disguise.

## Critical Overview

The wiki's single reading is [Miguel Marías's prologue (2026)](../sources/2026-04-13_powell-novela-marias.md). Marías — a Spanish film critic — frames the novel in relation to Powell's film career. His central argument: Powell's reputation as the visual craftsman of the Powell-Pressburger partnership (The Archers, 1941–1959), with Pressburger as the writer, is a myth. Powell directed before and after the collaboration; Pressburger's solo literary work was negligible. The novel is evidence of Powell's independent literary intelligence. Marías rates *A Waiting Game* as high as Powell's greatest films.

## Interpretive Controversies

### Controversy: Powell's role in Powell-Pressburger
- **Received position**: Pressburger wrote, Powell directed — a clean division of labour.
- **Marías's position** ([Marías, 2026](../sources/2026-04-13_powell-novela-marias.md)): This division is a myth not supported by the documented record. Powell was a filmmaker before and after the partnership; Pressburger's solo work does not support his reputation as the primary writer.
- **Status**: Marías's position stands uncontested in this wiki.
