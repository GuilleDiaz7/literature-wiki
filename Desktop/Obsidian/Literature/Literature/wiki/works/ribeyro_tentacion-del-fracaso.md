# La tentación del fracaso

## Bibliographic Summary

The complete diary of [Julio Ramón Ribeyro](../authors/ribeyro_julio-ramon.md), covering his adult life from Lima to Paris exile. Reviewed by [Matías Serra Bradford](../critics/serra-bradford_matias.md) in *Clarín* (Buenos Aires) in April 2026. Also: a volume of Ribeyro's stories reviewed in the same piece.

## Synopsis

A writer's journal kept over decades, from the 1950s through the 1990s. Ribeyro documents the daily life of the exile: literary ambitions and their disappointments, the textures of Paris poverty, friendship with other Latin American writers, the approach of illness and death. The title — "the temptation of failure" — names a psychic tendency Ribeyro is aware of in himself: the pull toward not-succeeding, the comfortable drama of the talented man who does not quite arrive.

## Critical Overview

[Serra Bradford](../critics/serra-bradford_matias.md): the diary is Ribeyro's most complete achievement — more so than the celebrated short stories, which are excellent but conventional in their virtues. The diary's texture is irreplaceable: the specific gravity of a specific life, the Peruvian exile's consciousness in Paris, the daily reckoning with failure. Serra Bradford's approach differs markedly from Torné: where Torné tends toward comparative hierarchies, Serra Bradford focuses on the phenomenology of reading — what happens when we encounter this particular voice.
