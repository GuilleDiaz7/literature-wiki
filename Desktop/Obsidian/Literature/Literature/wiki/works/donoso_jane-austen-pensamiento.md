## Synopsis

Donoso's essay takes on Jane Austen and, according to Torné, dismantles two prevalent clichés about her work with precision. The essay belongs to the tradition of novelists writing critically about other novelists — a genre where the practitioner's insight can cut through academic convention.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): uses Donoso's Austen essay as a springboard for his own reflections on "miradores del tiempo" — observation points on time, retrospection, and the novelist's craft. The Donoso essay is praised for its demolishing of two standard Austen clichés; Torné does not specify which two. The piece is as much Torné meditating on literary criticism itself as a review of Donoso.
