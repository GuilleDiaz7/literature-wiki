# Acerca del robo de historias y otros relatos

## Bibliographic Summary

Published in Spanish by Impedimenta, 160 pp. Translated by María Vútova. Third [Gueorgui Gospodínov](../authors/gospodinov_gueorgui.md) work in the wiki after *Física de la tristeza* and *El jardinero y la muerte*.

## Synopsis

A collection of brief, succinct stories that resist the standard norms of literary short fiction: no linear narration, no psychological connective tissue, no plot twists, no morals. The stories progress through unexpected associations and original compositional angles: a country's history told through an ear, life with one eye in the past and one in the future, the narrative voice transferred to the freshly massacred soul of a pig. The collection simulates a tradition of oral storytelling from which stories might descend into the book.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) positions the book generically: not poem, not drama, not novel, not chronicle, not essay or biography — positioned in the space of *cuento* and *relato*, though Gospodínov deliberately subverts that genre's most prevalent norms. His primary literary power here: the construction of witty, unexpected phrases. The humor is never pure comedy — shadows of political critique and resigned political resentment surface every few pages.
