# Mañana y tarde

## Bibliographic Summary

*Morgon og kveld* (2000). Published in Spanish by Nórdica / De Conatus, 112 pp. Trans. Cristina Gómez-Baggethun and Kirsti Baggethun. 18 €. Among the small corpus of Fosse work available in Spanish. Reviewed by Torné on the occasion of Fosse's 2023 Nobel Prize.

## Synopsis

Shows the first and last day of the life of the same man: Johannes, a fisherman. The first section depicts the hours before his birth (the body breathing empty of consciousness). The last section depicts the hours after his death — Johannes wanders, not quite understanding he is dead, meeting familiar figures from his past. Between these two poles, the middle of a life is glimpsed briefly: work, loves, marriage, the luck of living in a country with a welfare state ("Less good we already have our pension"). Whether the final section depicts supernatural transcendence or the last agitations of a subjectivity is deliberately left ambiguous.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): A Bernhard-like repetition syntax, perhaps closer to Biblical salmodias. The language never quite dances. But highly sensitive to special states (a new mother's gaze; a newborn's first hearings; the weight of everyday tools). The book's greatest virtue: daring to speak of everyday mysteries without intellectual sophistication — "the words of a fisherman, of a man Christ could have chosen to walk beside Him."

## Themes and Motifs

- Birth and death as symmetrical limits
- The everyday mystery of existence
- The difficulty of leaving the world (resistance of the quotidian)
- Welfare state and its implication in ordinary human dignity
