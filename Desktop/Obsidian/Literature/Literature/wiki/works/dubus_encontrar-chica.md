# Encontrar a una chica en América

## Bibliographic Summary

*Encontrar a una chica en América*. Trans. David Paradela López. Gallo Negro, 192 pp., €19. Reviewed by Torné in *El Periódico*, 3 April 2024. Note: four stories from this collection were excluded because Gallo Negro had already published them in a prior anthology (*Adulterio*); whether those were stronger is unknown.

## Synopsis

Stories and a title novella from the American working-class interior. The novella follows Hank, a middle-aged professor-writer, through successive loves; the stories work the same territory — men and women in post-industrial America, navigating violence, labor, desire. Dubus works in the tradition of Carver, Cheever, and Hemingway; the models surface openly (Conrad for one story, Lawrence for another).

## Critical Overview

[Torné, 2024](../sources/2024-04-03_encontrar-chica-dubus.md): the collection is competent but limited. Torné's frame is polemical: American narrative fiction has become a "mine of precious metals" whose poetics — hyper-realism, emotional compression, colourless description — has been so internalized it passes for quality when it is merely a standard toolset. Dubus fits the template. The title novella specifically lacks the psychological deepening a longer form demands. Torné also notes the reflexive invocation of Chekhov to legitimize US short fiction, when none of these authors actually resembles Chekhov.
