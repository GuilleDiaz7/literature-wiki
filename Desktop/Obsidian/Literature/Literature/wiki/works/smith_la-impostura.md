# La impostura

## Bibliographic Summary

Spanish edition: *La impostura*. Trans. Eugenia Vázquez Nacarino. Salamandra, 480 pp., €24. Original English title: *The Fraud* (2023). First novel by [Zadie Smith](../authors/smith_zadie.md) set entirely in the Victorian period.

## Synopsis

The novel follows Eliza Touchet, whose curiosity drives a double plot interweaving a minor Victorian literary figure with the ego of a thoroughbred, and a legal imbroglio where nobody is who they seem. Set in Victorian England. The title's imposture floats across characters — a dancing signifier applicable to multiple figures in the narrative. (Source: [Torné 2024](../sources/2024-11-14_la-impostura-smith.md))

## Critical Overview

[Gonzalo Torné's 2024 review](../sources/2024-11-14_la-impostura-smith.md) frames the novel within the sub-genre of contemporary British Victorian fiction — a rite of passage for British novelists (McEwan, Barnes, Ishiguro). Smith deploys two contemporary structural choices: (1) construction from very short units — almost comic-strip pieces — compressed by humour and separated by ellipses; (2) a symbolic dimension requiring interpretive keys beyond the action, centred on the floating signifier "imposture."

Torné's verdict is mixed. He praises the dancing signifier and the ambition of applying the double-plot device contemporarily. His reservation: a mismatch between the materials deployed, the technique, and the book's purposes; the brief-unit approach becomes repetitive, and the novel "closes somewhat falsely." He explicitly declines to call it too long — "a pretentious licence for any critic facing a writer of this stature" — but implies it.
