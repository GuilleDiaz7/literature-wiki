# Correspondencia Flaubert-Baudelaire

## Bibliographic Summary

Correspondence between Flaubert and Baudelaire. Edited by Ignacio Echevarría. Published by Alpha Decay. Reviewed by Torné in June 2023.

## Synopsis

The letters exchanged between Flaubert and Baudelaire during the critical years when both were navigating the aftermath of their respective trials (*Madame Bovary* and *Les Fleurs du Mal*, both prosecuted for obscenity in 1857). The correspondence documents a friendship between two writers who recognized each other's projects without being entirely sympathetic to each other's methods. The letters touch on aesthetics, on the legal persecution, on personal life, on the literary scene of Second Empire France.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): Ignacio Echevarría's editing is attentive and well-framed. The Alpha Decay publication is characteristic of that press's commitment to carefully produced literary documents. The correspondence illuminates both writers through contrast: Flaubert's prose-as-architecture against Baudelaire's prose-as-intoxication; the impersonality of *Bovary* against the subjectivity of *Fleurs*. Torné reads the letters as a document of how two writers of the first rank regarded each other.
