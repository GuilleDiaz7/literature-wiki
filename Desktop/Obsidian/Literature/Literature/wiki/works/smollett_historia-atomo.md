# Historia y aventuras de un átomo

## Bibliographic Summary

*The History and Adventures of an Atom* (1769). Reviewed by Torné in June 2024 in a Spanish-language edition. Late Smollett, written in his final years.

## Synopsis

An atom narrates its passage through various human bodies across history, including prominent figures in British political life. The conceit allows Smollett to conduct political satire through radical displacement: political actions and their perpetrators are seen from the inside (literally, from within human bodies), stripped of dignity. The atom moves from intestine to brain, carrying its satirical eye throughout.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): the talking-atom conceit opens a novelistic possibility that the novel then abandons — the point of view is eventually subordinated to conventional satirical procedure. Smollett fails to exploit the formal radicalism of his premise: the atom's perspective could defamiliarize political reality completely, but the satire reverts to recognizable 18th-century modes. A wasted opportunity of the first order.
