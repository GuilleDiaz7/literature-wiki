# Sé mía

## Bibliographic Summary

*Be Mine* (2023). Fifth novel in the Frank Bascombe series, following *The Sportswriter* (1986), *Independence Day* (1995, Pulitzer), *The Lay of the Land* (2006), and *Let Me Be Frank With You* (2014). Reviewed by Torné in June 2024.

## Synopsis

Frank Bascombe, now in his late seventies, takes his son Paul — who has ALS — on a road trip to Mount Rushmore. The novel is a final confrontation with mortality: the dying son, the aging father, the American landscape. The "road trip" functions as a classic American literary form, here stripped of its expansionary energy and turned toward diminishment. Bascombe's voice — his verbal technique of managing distance through language — is tested against the proximity of death.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): Ford achieves here the "atone" prose of late style — a measured, deliberate language that has internalized its own elegiac knowledge. This is not the autumnal sentimentality of lesser writers but something harder: a prose that has earned its quiet by absorbing the full weight of an American life across five novels. The ALS material is handled without false consolation.
