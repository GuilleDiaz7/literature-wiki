# Bruja Luna, Rey Araña

## Bibliographic Summary

*Moon Witch, Spider King* (2022) is the second volume of [Marlon James](../authors/james_marlon.md)'s Dark Star trilogy, mixing fantasy with African mythology.

## Synopsis

The Moon Witch (Sogolon), a sorceress nearly 200 years old, narrates how she failed in the events of the first novel (*Black Leopard, Red Wolf*) and recounts her prolonged battle against Aesi — a figure representing tyranny and the yoke of obedience. Where she was the villain of the first book, here she is the protagonist, revealed as an indomitable woman with her own arc.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): an improvement over the first volume — more cohesive, better protagonist, clearer narrative conduit. But James's fundamental problem remains: unresolved tension between playing the genre straight and using it for more complex literary purposes. His symbolism is too transparent; violence is extreme and lacks depth (compared to an adolescent tantrum). Prose "tries to fly but cannot take off" — like flightless birds (ostrich, emu, penguin). Not comparable to Ishiguro's use of genre (*Cuando fuimos huérfanos*).

## Interpretive Controversies

### Controversy: Genre vs. Literary Ambition in Dark Fantasy

- **Position A** (Torné): James cannot choose between genre fiction and literary ambition; the result satisfies neither fully.
- **Position B** (implied general reception): The Booker Prize for *Seven Killings* and the Dark Star trilogy's ambition represent a significant literary project rooted in African mythology.
- **Status**: No other critical voice in this wiki on James.

## Themes and Motifs

- African mythology and fantasy genre
- Tyranny and female resistance
- Violence in fiction: decorative vs. purposeful

## Intertextual Connections

- [Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md) compares James unfavorably to Ishiguro's *Cuando fuimos huérfanos* (science fiction as devastating political novel)
- Comparison with Tolkien (James's violence is less instrumental than Tolkien's battle scenes)
