# Cartas I

## Bibliographic Summary

First volume of Lovecraft's selected letters in Spanish translation. Edited and translated by Javier Calvo. Published by [publisher not specified in source]. Reviewed by Torné in March 2023.

## Synopsis

A selection of Lovecraft's correspondence, the first volume covering his early life and the formation of his literary ideas. Lovecraft was a prolific letter-writer — his correspondence runs to thousands of pages — and the letters function as a parallel autobiography and aesthetic manifesto. Key themes in this volume: his self-formation as a writer, his theories of "lo extraño" (the weird), his reading in Gothic and Romantic literature, and his personal relationships within the nascent weird fiction community.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): the letters are essential for understanding the theoretical underpinning of Lovecraft's fiction — particularly his essay "Supernatural Horror in Literature" and its relation to his creative practice. Javier Calvo's editorial work is noted as careful and well-framed. Torné registers the standard critical difficulty: Lovecraft's racism and its complex relationship to the cosmic indifference he theorized.
