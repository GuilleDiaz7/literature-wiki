# Ciudad Victoria

## Bibliographic Summary

*Victory City* (2023). Published post-fatwa, after Rushdie's 2022 attack and recovery. Reviewed by Torné in early 2023.

## Synopsis

A goddess-touched woman narrates the rise and fall of an empire over several centuries. The frame is explicitly Sheherazade-like: a storyteller weaving a kingdom into existence through the power of words alone. The novel operates in the register of the legendary and the mythological, drawing on South Asian epic traditions.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): Rushdie at his most characteristic — the Sheherazade mode functioning as both structure and subject. The comparison with García Márquez is inevitable and double-edged: Rushdie's magic is more overtly programmatic, less organically embedded in local reality than Macondo's magic. Torné notes the post-fatwa biographical context without subordinating the literary assessment to it.
