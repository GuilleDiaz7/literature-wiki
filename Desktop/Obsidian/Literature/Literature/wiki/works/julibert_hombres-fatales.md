# Hombres fatales

## Bibliographic Summary

Essay by [Elisenda Julibert](../authors/julibert_elisenda.md) on the figure of the *homme fatal* — the male equivalent of the femme fatale — in literature and culture. Reviewed by Torné in November 2022.

## Synopsis

A systematic demolition and inversion: the femme fatale is a cultural construct that has obscured an equally operative male archetype. Julibert traces the *homme fatal* through literary and cultural history, using theoretical frameworks drawn from Girard (mimetic desire), Barthes (mythology and naturalization), and Rosset (reality and its double). The essay argues that the male "fatal" figure has been rendered invisible by the ideological work that overdetermined the femme fatale.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): an impressive methodological toolkit — Girard, Barthes, Rosset deployed with precision rather than as decoration. The inversion of the femme fatale paradigm is genuinely illuminating. Julibert's essay belongs to the tradition of rigorous cultural critique that uses literary examples as evidence for structural arguments. A serious intervention in the field.
