# Diario de sueños

## Bibliographic Summary

Second volume in the Javier Calvo-edited Lovecraft project, following *Cartas I*. Lovecraft's dream journal in Spanish translation. Reviewed by Torné in May 2024.

## Synopsis

Lovecraft kept a journal of his dreams — vivid, atmospheric, sometimes architecturally elaborate — that served as a direct quarry for his fiction. The dream journal reads as pre-text: the raw material from which the fiction was to be refined. In this sense the dreams are more than biographical curiosity; they are the evidence of the process by which "lo extraño" moved from unconscious experience to literary category.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): the dream journal reveals the fundamental paradox of Lovecraft — that the fiction ultimately falls short of the dreams. The unconscious material is richer, stranger, more ungraspable than anything the prose finally manages. The journal thus functions as a critique of the fiction by excess: the imagination producing more than the technique can hold.
