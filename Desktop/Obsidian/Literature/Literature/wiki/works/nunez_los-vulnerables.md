## Synopsis

During Covid-19 lockdown, an older woman with literary ambitions shares a small flat with a young vegan she has never met, and an imposing parrot. The novel opens with loosely connected memories of lost friendships before settling into this cohabitation. The texture is more essayistic than dramatic: the narrator meditates on vulnerability, fragile connections, and the emotional registers — tenderness, empathy, sudden affinity — that survive confinement. (Source: [Torné 2024](../sources/2024-10-01_los-vulnerables-nunez.md))

## Critical Overview

The wiki's single reading is [Gonzalo Torné's 2024 review](../sources/2024-10-01_los-vulnerables-nunez.md). Torné characterises the style as schematic, allusive, learned, and analytical — prose more interested in presenting lives as summaries and examples than in extensive narration. He frames this deliberate sparseness as appropriate to the novel's subject: the nuances of delicacy, the underattended emotions of tenderness, trust, responsibility, and care.

Torné places Nunez within a growing literary current that approaches the present as an open debate about how to live together — not to expose base passions but to explore more delicate, unattended emotional registers. He compares her project to Ali Smith's (though distinct: Smith favours political recuperation, Nunez favours vulnerability). His praise is unqualified: the novel is "concentrated on something smaller but more decisive — the nuances of delicacy."
