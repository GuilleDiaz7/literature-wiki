# Salir a robar caballos

## Bibliographic Summary

*Ut og stjæle hester* (2003). Norwegian novel. Published in Spanish translation as *Salir a robar caballos*. Trans. Kirsti Baggethun and Asunción Lorenzo. Reviewed by Torné in late 2022.

## Synopsis

A man in his sixties withdraws to an isolated cabin in the Norwegian woods after his wife's death. Memories of a summer at age fifteen — when he and a friend "went out stealing horses" — surface and interweave with the present. The novel manages temporal layers with geometric precision: past and present coexist without contaminating each other, each preserving its full weight.

## Critical Overview

[Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md): places this novel at the top of his comparative hierarchy for the period — above Houellebecq, Cărtărescu, and Franzen. The distinguishing achievement: time handled as a geometric form rather than a mere psychological backdrop. Petterson gives each temporal layer its full integrity; the past does not explain or redeem the present but simply coexists with it in the same space of consciousness.
