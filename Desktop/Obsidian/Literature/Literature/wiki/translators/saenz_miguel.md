# Miguel Sáenz

## Profile

Spanish translator from German, known primarily as the most constant translator of Thomas Bernhard into Spanish and as one of the foremost literary translators working in the German-Spanish language pair. Associated with major Spanish literary publishers including Alfaguara. [unsourced — verify: further biographical and professional detail not available in current wiki sources.]

## Critical Attention

[Gonzalo Torné](../critics/torne_gonzalo.md), in his review of [*Andar* by Thomas Bernhard](../works/bernhard_andar.md) ([source](../sources/2025-06-28_andar-bernhard.md)), makes an unusually strong claim about Sáenz:

> "Quizá el único escritor cuya prosa se benefició del virus fue su traductor más constante: Miguel Sáenz."

The "Bernhard virus" is Torné's metaphor for the stylistic infection that spread through Spanish literature when Bernhard's prose became known — an infection nearly impossible to bear without falling into parody, since Bernhard (like Borges) resists imitation. Torné's claim is that Sáenz is the *sole* writer whose own prose genuinely benefited from this exposure.

This is a remarkable critical position: Sáenz is not praised for producing accurate translations, but for something rarer — for being creatively and literarily transformed by his encounter with Bernhard's style, in a way that is visible in the quality of his translated prose. The implication is that Sáenz's translations of Bernhard are not merely accurate but stylistically alive — that they absorb Bernhard's sadistic echo-chamber without collapsing into caricature.

Note: The specific edition of *Andar* reviewed (Trans. Virginia Maza, Contraseña) is *not* translated by Sáenz. Torné's remark is about Sáenz as a broader phenomenon — the Bernhard translator par excellence — not about the book under review.
